## Rationale per bundle

- **The Birth Day Bundle: Everything for the Hospital Bag and the Worry Before It** ($29): One genuine moment: you are packing the bag and you are scared. P14 birth plan, P27 hospital bag, and P51 the safety questions are the practical spine. P52 names the fear out loud, and the B06 ebook rides along as the bedtime read. Four printables at $49 a-la-carte, set to $29 (59%) so the saving is obvious without feeling like a fire sale.
- **The Fourth Trimester Bundle: The First Six Weeks Home, Set on the Counter** ($41): This is the cart a newborn parent actually needs in week one: P07 survival planner, P11 the sleep and feed log, P34 cue cards for the crying, P38 freezer meals you eat with one hand, and P15 the door sign that handles visitors. B10 ebook included as the read for the 3am feed. $65 a-la-carte to $41 (63%). The broadest, most-gifted everyday bundle.
- **The Postpartum Mind Bundle: For the Racing Nights and the Mom Rage You Did Not Expect** ($36): The mental-health cluster sells one at a time, but the person buying P09 usually also needs P21 for the anxiety, P23 for the anger no one warned them about, and P29 for the who-am-I-now question underneath all of it. B07 ebook included. Kept honestly modest in voice. $59 to $36 (61%).
- **For the Partner Bundle: The Nine Months and the Six Weeks You Are Also Living** ($25): A clean partner arc: P55 covers the pregnancy, P04 covers the postpartum weeks, and P15 gives them a concrete job (run the door, the meal train, the how-to-help list). B08 ebook included as the playbook in their pocket. Three printables, $39 to $25 (64%). Reads as a real gift, not a stocking filler.
- **The Feeding Bundle: Latch, First Spoon, and the Pump Schedule, All in One Place** ($33): Feeding is not one decision, it is a year of them. P12 gets the latch sorted, P11 tracks the foggy early weeks, P17 handles the return-to-work pump plan, and P22 carries it through to solids. Spans the full feeding timeline so it keeps earning its place months after purchase. $54 to $33 (61%).
- **The Big Feelings Bundle: What to Say on the Kitchen Floor** ($30): The toddler-emotions parent is buying scripts because they are out of words in the moment. P13 cards name the feeling, P16 covers the tantrum, P30 covers the sibling fight, and P24 covers the bedtime hand-off. B04 ebook included. A tight, same-buyer set. $50 to $30 (60%).
- **The Hard Conversations Bundle: Saying No, Asking for Help, and Surviving the Holiday Table** ($30): Boundary scripts are the brand's sleeper hit and they pair naturally. P02 handles the visitor, P49 handles asking for the help you actually need, P25 handles the holiday table, and P15 puts the boundary on the front door. B01 ebook included. The whole social-pressure load in one zip. $47 to $30 (64%).
- **The Back to Work Bundle: The First 90 Days After Leave** ($35): Return-to-work is a dense, anxious window where people buy several things at once. P31 plans the 90 days, P17 sorts the pumping, P32 hands off to the sitter or daycare, and P29 sits with the matrescence question that the return surfaces. $59 to $35 (59%). High intent, high basket.
- **The Trying Bundle: Cycle Notes, One Full IVF Round, and the Paperwork of Adoption** ($29): These buyers are underserved and loyal. P41 is the discreet daily tracker, P35 carries a full treatment cycle, and P48 covers the adoption and home-study route for the pregnancy that does not show. Grouping them respects that the path is rarely linear. $46 to $29 (63%).
- **The Second Story Bundle: Pregnancy After Loss, Held Beside the First** ($28): P20 holds the grief of the loss, P40 is the planner for the rainbow pregnancy, and P53 carries the trimester-by-trimester emotional weight. The same person often needs all three, in sequence. Priced gently on purpose for a tender buyer. $44 to $28 (64%).
- **The Two at Once Bundle: Twin Pregnancy, the First Six Weeks of Two, and the Big Sibling** ($29): Twin parents are an obvious cluster the wider market ignores. P33 covers the twin pregnancy, P45 covers the first six weeks home with two, and P19 prepares an older sibling for the arrival. A niche set with strong word-of-mouth in twin groups. $46 to $29 (63%).
- **The Caregiving Bundle: Aging Parents, the First 90 Days and the Long Stretch After** ($25): The eldercare line extends the brand past babies into the other tender stretch. P46 handles the acute first 90 days, P50 handles the long haul, and P49 gives them the words to ask for help. B11 and B12 ebooks included as the companion reads. $40 to $25 (62%).
- **The Pregnancy Keepsake Bundle: The Weeks, the Feelings, and the Letters You Write Along the Way** ($33): Keepsake buyers want the record and the feeling together. P08 is the week-by-week memory book, P56 is the letters-to-baby journal, P53 holds the emotions, and P51 answers the safety questions that come up the whole way through. A warm, giftable spine. $56 to $33 (59%).
- **The Complete Pregnancy and Postpartum Library: The Whole Spine, From the Plus Sign to the Other Side of Six Weeks** ($65): The high-price anchor, set on purpose at $65 so every single and every smaller bundle reads as a bargain next to it. Six of the strongest, broadest heroes: P08 the pregnancy, P52 the birth fear, P07 the newborn weeks, P09 the mood, P38 the food, and P04 the partner. At 73% of the $89 a-la-carte it still shows a clear $24 saving, but its real job is to be the most expensive thing on the shelf. Marked as the megabundle anchor.
