# Which One Do I Need? — Buyer Routing Guide

Drop this on the site as a quiz or decision table. Maps a buyer's situation to the one product they need.

| When the buyer says... | Point them to | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "I am angry and ashamed of how I snapped at my kid, and the apple juice was not really the reason" | **P23** | The Mom Rage Workbook maps what is actually under the anger and gives you the repair script for after the yelling, which is the part no one teaches. |
| "I cannot stop checking the baby's breathing and I refuse to sleep in case I am the only one who would notice" | **P21** | The Postpartum Anxiety Companion is the noticing log and grounding cards for the racing nights, plus the list of signs that mean call today. |
| "I think this might be more than the baby blues and I want to track it so I can describe it to a provider" | **P09** | The Postpartum Depression and Anxiety Journal is the first-year mood tracker built to hand to a real provider, where P21 is narrower and aimed at the anxiety-and-checking pattern. |
| "I look in the mirror and do not recognize myself and people keep telling me to get my old self back" | **P29** | The Who Am I Now journal is prompted writing for meeting the new self instead of chasing the old one, no mood-tracking and no fixing. |
| "I am burnt out on planners that want ten to-dos a day and a morning routine I will never keep" | **P03** | The Slow Motherhood planner is the undated anti-productivity one with three list slots a day and two questions, not a habit tracker. |
| "The doorbell keeps ringing and my mother-in-law keeps giving advice and I do not have the words to push back" | **P02** | The Visitor Boundary Scripts give you exact lines for family, in-laws, and advice-givers, so you are not inventing them at the door. |
| "I am dreading the holiday table where every deferred comment about my body and my parenting lands at once" | **P25** | The Holiday Boundary Scripts are built for the gathering you have to sit at, with grounding cards and a drive-home debrief, where P02 is for everyday visitors. |
| "People keep saying let me know if you need anything and I never know how to actually ask" | **P49** | The Asking for Help scripts give specific requests to specific people, since the difference between a yes and a no is the specificity, not the relationship. |
| "People are coming to meet the baby and I want them to bring a meal and not expect to be hosted" | **P15** | The Visitor Welcome Packet is the door sign plus a how-to-help list and meal-train page, so the help is organized before anyone arrives. |
| "My toddler is melting down on the floor right now and I am barely holding on myself" | **P16** | The Tantrum Scripts are six-second lines you can say mid-meltdown with your own nervous system half offline, no framework to remember. |
| "After the storm my kid still cannot tell me what was wrong and I want to build that vocabulary over time" | **P13** | The Big Feelings cards are the after-the-storm vocabulary you lay on the floor to ask was it any of these, where P16 is for the live moment. |
| "My kids will not stop fighting with each other and I run out of calm things to say" | **P30** | The Sibling Fighting Scripts are forty calm lines for the referee moments, aimed at conflict between kids rather than one child's meltdown. |
| "I am pregnant for the first time and I want one keepsake to hold the weeks and the appointments" | **P08** | The Pregnancy Journal week by week is the first-time-mom keepsake from weeks six to forty, where P51 is a reference and P53 is an emotions journal. |
| "I keep wondering is this safe and I forget half my questions by the time I see my OB" | **P51** | The Is It Safe During Pregnancy reference organizes the questions by category so you walk in with the list, no opinions and no medicine names. |
| "I am genuinely scared to give birth and trust your body has not helped at all" | **P52** | The Fear of Childbirth Workbook treats the fear as information and gives it somewhere to go, plus scripts for your provider and partner. |
| "The feelings of this pregnancy are big and I want somewhere to put them trimester by trimester" | **P53** | The Pregnancy Emotions Journal is prenatal prompts across the three trimesters, where P52 targets birth fear specifically. |
| "I am pregnant and have to tell my boss and figure out leave and the handoff at work" | **P54** | The Maternity Leave Planner has the HR scripts, the telling-your-boss language, and the handoff template for pregnant-at-work logistics. |
| "I need to write down what I want for labor and a one-page version a nurse can actually read" | **P14** | The Birth Plan Template is the fillable worksheet pack with a C-section option and a one-page nurse summary, the document itself. |
| "My due date is close and I have no idea what to actually pack for the hospital" | **P27** | The Hospital Bag Checklist covers mom, partner, and baby in one list so the packing is done and not guessed at 2am. |
| "I am planning a vaginal birth after a cesarean and need the questions and the decision log for my doctor" | **P39** | The VBAC Prep Planner is the printable TOLAC and repeat-cesarean planner with the questions to ask, which a general birth plan does not cover. |
| "I just had a C-section and the recovery is its own thing week by week" | **P06** | The C-Section Recovery Planner tracks healing across weeks one to six, built for surgical recovery rather than general postpartum. |
| "It is the foggy first weeks and I cannot remember the last feed or which side or the last diaper" | **P07** | The Newborn Feeding and Diaper Log is the first-six-weeks daily tracker with a whats-normal reference for the sleep-deprived brain. |
| "Breastfeeding hurts and the latch feels wrong and I do not know what I am doing" | **P12** | The Breastfeeding Troubleshooting Guide is the latch checklist, positions, and combo-feeding help, the how-to rather than the log. |
| "I am going back to work and have to figure out pumping around my actual workday" | **P17** | The Return to Work Pumping Planner is the pump schedule for the day before and every day after, where P31 is the broader return plan. |
| "My baby is ready for first foods and I am nervous about allergens and choking" | **P22** | The Starting Solids Planner has the readiness signs, the first-foods plan, and an allergen log for the first spoon. |
| "My baby sleeps all day and is awake all night and I cannot see any pattern" | **P36** | The Sleep Regression Journal is a two-week tracker to surface the night-waking pattern, a logging tool and deliberately not a method. |
| "I am doing this on my own and every planner assumes a partner I do not have" | **P26** | The Single Mom Postpartum Planner never assumes a second adult and names the help you actually have by name instead of the word partner. |
| "We are having twins and the regular pregnancy planners do not fit what is happening to me" | **P33** | The Twin Pregnancy Planner is week by week for the pregnancy with more appointments and different growth that single-baby planners skip. |

| "It has been a long time now and the caregiving is my second job and I am running out" | **P50** | The Long Caregiving Stretch workbook is built for sustainability and the honest pages, where P46 is for the first ninety days of shock. |
| "I am quietly tracking my body to conceive and I do not want a cover that announces it to the office" | **P41** | The Discreet Fertility Tracker reads as a plain daily notebook with neutral field labels and a decode page you can tear out. |
| "I am in the middle of an IVF cycle with the shots and the two week wait and the beta day" | **P35** | The IVF Planner is the cycle tracker for stims, the wait, and beta day, where P41 is for quieter at-home cycle notes. |

| "A new baby is coming and my older kid is going to be thrown by it" | **P19** | The Preparing Older Sibling pack has the scripts, a hospital-day plan, and regression help for the bigger one. |
