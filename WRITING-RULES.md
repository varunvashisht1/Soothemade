# Soothemade care contract

> The standard for any Soothemade content that ships to a buyer: PDFs (`products/Pnn_*/content.md` + `web.md`), site copy, journal essays, blog posts, marketing collateral. Does **not** apply to: code comments, commit messages, `README.md`, internal `STATUS.md` / `PLAN.md`, terminal output.
>
> This file is **the care contract**, not a style guide. Soothemade asks people to pay $14 for a PDF in a season of life when they are tired, often grieving, often unseen. The contract is: that PDF must read as if a real person made it, for them, knowing what they are in. If it does not pass that bar, it does not ship.
>
> `AS_BUILT.md` Sections 4 and 5 cover brand voice and punctuation hygiene. This file is the contract those rules serve.

---

## The bar

A new parent buys this at 11 p.m. while breastfeeding on a phone. The product arrives in their email. They open it on a tablet between feeds. They have $14 less in their account and roughly six minutes before the next thing happens.

**In those six minutes, the product must do one thing: make them feel that a person made this, on purpose, for them.**

Not "made by AI but voiced warm." Not "a generic printable with sage palette." A person, who has been where they are, made this specific thing because the specific thing was missing. That has to land in the first page. If it doesn't, the rest of the catalog can be perfect and the brand still fails.

Everything below is in service of that bar.

## The six care signals

A product is shipped only when it passes all six. Some are auto-checkable. Some are gut checks. All six are non-negotiable.

### Signal 1 — Scene anchor in the opening note

**The opening note must contain at least one concrete scene marker in the first ten lines.** A time of day, a room, a body sensation, a specific gesture, an overheard line. The reader needs proof that a person was somewhere when they wrote this.

✅ *Crafted:*
- *"I'd say 'I'm fine' to the pediatrician and mean it. Then I'd cry in the parking lot and not know why."* (P09)
- *"I am someone who has been in a quiet room with a wand-and-screen and watched the technician's face go still."* (P20)
- *"I screamed at my three-year-old in the laundry room. The reason was not, in fact, the spilled apple juice. It was the third time that hour I had cleaned the apple juice."* (P23)
- *"I am writing this at 5:47 a.m. The light outside the window is doing the yellow thing."* (umbrella)

❌ *AI-mill:*
- *"Postpartum is a challenging journey. This planner offers tools to support your recovery."*
- *"Many parents find the first weeks overwhelming. We designed this to help."*

**If the opening could have been generated by summarizing the topic from a Wikipedia article, it fails this signal.** The fix is a single concrete scene. One time-of-day, one room, one body sensation, one overheard line. That is the price of admission.

### Signal 2 — The voice has a face

**Every "A note from me" / "A note before you start" / "A note from the maker" section ends with `*With care, Maya*`** (or, for the umbrella, `*with care, the studio*`).

The brand mark `Soothemade` lives on the cover and the colophon — template-driven, not in the personal note. Personal notes are signed by the person who wrote them.

This single character matters more than it looks. Anonymous-first-person voices read as brand voices. A name, even a quiet one, makes the reader hear a person speaking. Maya is the named voice of the Notes line (see `brand/persona.md`, locked as Option B). When future sub-lines (Kitchen, Studio, Field) launch, each gets its own named voice — the umbrella keeps "the studio."

### Signal 3 — The disclaimer is voiced, not armored

YMYL products must carry a not-medical-advice disclaimer. **The disclaimer cannot read as legal armor.** It must do the legal work *and* be the brand voice. The same sentence carries both jobs.

✅ *Voiced (P09):*
> *"A journal is for noticing patterns. It is not for crisis. If you are in crisis, you need a human, not a page."*

✅ *Voiced (P38):*
> *"This is a cookbook, not medical advice. Allergies and dietary restrictions in your household are between you and your provider. Adjust freely."*

❌ *Armored (any product that reuses the standard block verbatim):*
> *"This is a planning tool, not medical advice. For specific concerns about your [topic], please contact your [provider]. We do not name medications..."*

If a disclaimer appears word-for-word in five products, four of those products have not voiced theirs. The pattern in P09 and P38 shows what voiced disclaimers look like: short, direct, owned by the writer, often funny in the way a tired friend is funny.

### Signal 4 — The product is modest about what it does

**Soothemade never promises transformation, healing on a timeline, or a "you'll feel amazing" outcome.** The brand voice is *"a slightly-older friend who's been through it, telling you the truth no one else does."* A friend does not over-promise.

✅ *Modest:*
- *"Not to fix anything. Just to keep a record."* (P09)
- *"The kid will not remember any of it. You will."* (P43)
- *"This is the structure. It does not tell you which decisions to make. It is the place to write them down."* (P46)

❌ *Over-promising:*
- *"Transform your postpartum experience."*
- *"Heal your relationship with food in 30 days."*
- *"Unlock the calm you deserve."*

Banned words in customer-facing copy: `transform`, `heal` (as a promise verb — describing a process is fine), `unlock`, `deserve` (you-deserve framing), `journey`, `empowered`, `breakthrough`, `radical`, `level up`, `next level`. Also see the full hygiene list in the appendix.

The test: if the marketing copy could appear on a $300 wellness app, it has overpromised. Soothemade is a $14 PDF, and the modesty is the brand.

### Signal 5 — Hard things are named, not euphemized

**The brand promise is to say the thing other people sanitize.** Use the plain word.

✅ *Named:*
- *"mom rage"* (not "challenging emotions")
- *"loss"* (not "what happened" or "your journey")
- *"the primary witness of someone else's diminishment"* (P46 eldercare)
- *"the day you found out"* (P56 letters-to-baby)
- *"the bravest sentence in postpartum motherhood is 'I am not okay'"* (P09)

❌ *Sanitized:*
- *"navigating big feelings"* (just say what feeling)
- *"the unexpected outcome"* (loss, miscarriage, stillbirth — say which)
- *"a new chapter"* (say what is starting and what is ending)

The voice does not avoid hard nouns. It does not soften the body. It does not pretend the room is not the room. Lochia, bleeding, the night feed, the parking-lot cry — all of these are nameable.

This signal is also the partner of the YMYL discipline. Naming a feeling is not a medical claim. Naming a body part is not prescription. The brand can be plain and still be legally safe — those are not in conflict.

### Signal 6 — Each product is its own shape

**The catalog cannot read as 55 instances of one template.** Variation of form is what hand-made shelves feel like.

The default pattern that has accumulated:
1. Title
2. *Tagline*
3. `## Read this first` or `## A note before you start`
4. `## What this [is] is, and is not` (IS / IS NOT bullets)
5. `## How to use this [product]`
6. Body sections

This pattern is fine for 30 of 55 products. The other 25 should reach for the form their content wants. A keepsake journal does not need an IS / IS NOT block. A 24-page recipe collection does not. A scripts pack does not. A permission-slips page (like P43's "you are allowed to skip the smash cake") does the same expectation-setting work as IS / IS NOT, in voice.

**Catalog-wide audit:** if more than 50% of products open with the same H2 structure, the catalog is templated. Vary it.

## The audit

`scripts/audit_writing_rules.py` measures all six signals. Output is a per-product care scorecard (six checks, each pass / fail / human-review) plus catalog-wide patterns (template-frequency, disclaimer-reuse). Read-only. Run before any PDF render batch:

```bash
py scripts/audit_writing_rules.py
```

Some signals are auto-checkable (signature presence, banned-word hits, scene-anchor regex). Others (modesty, hard-things-named) flag candidates for a human pass. The auditor never says "this product is good." It says "this product passed the mechanical checks and here is what still needs a human to read."

## When to break a signal

The six signals are non-negotiable for SHIPPED products. They can be broken in process:

- **Signal 1 (scene anchor):** A pure-utility product (a hospital-bag checklist, a babysitter handoff pack) may not need a scene-anchored note. It can open with a sentence of voice and move into the form. Acceptable. The other five signals still apply.
- **Signal 6 (form variety):** A first draft can copy the template. The reviewer asks "could this be the shape its content wants?" before ship.

The other four signals (voice has a face, disclaimer is voiced, modest, hard-things-named) are never broken. If a product wants to break them, it is the wrong product.

## Appendix — punctuation and AI-tell hygiene

These are the hygiene rules below the care signals. Enforced by `scripts/scrub_ai_punctuation.py` (auto-fix for punctuation) and surfaced as warnings by the auditor (vocabulary).

### Punctuation (hard, auto-fixed)

- ❌ No em-dashes (`—`). Use commas or periods.
- ❌ No semicolons (`;`). Use periods.
- ✅ Curly quotes, hyphens, en dashes (sparingly), ellipses (sparingly).

### Banned vocabulary (hard, surfaced by auditor)

AI-mill smell:
```
delve / delving, leverage, robust, seamless, navigate the,
in conclusion, moreover, furthermore, in summary, synergy,
dive deep, embark, unlock, ecosystem, at the heart of,
stands as, journey
```

Wellness-overpromise smell (Soothemade-specific, see Signal 4):
```
transform, heal (as promise verb), unlock, deserve,
empowered, breakthrough, radical, level up, next level
```

Filler:
```
ultimately, at the end of the day (filler use only —
literal time-of-day uses stay), needless to say,
in order to → to, due to the fact that → because,
going forward → from here, in terms of → drop
```

Brand-specific avoid (from `brand/voice-guide.md`):
```
mama, mommy, bump, kiddo, little one, blessed, cherish,
enjoy every moment, crush it, #momlife, supermom,
"studies show" / "experts say" without citing,
should / must / have to → "might want to"
```

### Carve-outs

- **`actually`** is voice-defining for Soothemade. Kept. ("What actually moves supply", "the days the system does not actually accommodate you".)
- **`harness` as a physical noun** is allowed (car-seat harness, high-chair harness). Banned only as verb / metaphor.
- **`at the end of the day`** as literal time stays. Banned only as filler ("ultimately").
- **`heal` describing a process** is allowed ("the body heals at its own pace"). Banned as a promise verb on marketing ("we will heal your postpartum").
- **American spelling** held — audience is US new parents. British spelling would clash.

### Density (hard)

- Prose paragraphs ≤ 600 chars (tracking grids, fill-in templates, ASCII-bordered forms exempt — they are designed visual artifacts).
- Sentences ≤ 50 words. Natural splits: `, and` / `, but` / `, because`.
