Soothemade · shop-window direction · variations

Five variations on the shop-window shape.

Each varies the tiles, the hero, or both — not just colors. Same brand theme, same content. Open each, react.

Locked across all five: type stack (Fraunces, Inter, Caveat), the daylight palette family, the four rooms (Notes open, Kitchen/Studio/Field building), the locked tagline. What shifts: how the rooms are visually arranged, what the hero is, where the visual weight sits.

01.
Refined original

Same shape, tighter palette and a richer window.

Cleanup pass on the first shop-window. Replaced the scattered tile palette (terracotta + wheat + sage + black) with a more cohesive warm-vintage set — terracotta, mustard ochre, deep moss, deep umber. All in the same family.

What changes Hero window is now a layered composition (color block + bigger botanical SVG + caption strip below), not one flat sage rectangle. Room tile palette is more cohesive.

02.
The shelf

Rooms as book spines on a shelf.

The four rooms become tall narrow vertical "spines" side by side, like books on a shelf. Each one shows its name running vertically in Caveat. Hover lifts the spine off the shelf. Small captions live below the shelf.

What changes Hero becomes a centered text intro (no right-side window). The rooms section is the visual moment — a charcoal "shelf board" with 4 colored spines you can pull from.

03.
Featured Notes hero

The open room IS the hero.

Notes takes over the whole top of the page as a full-bleed terracotta scene. The studio intro is a small strip below. The other three rooms sit lower as smaller, calmer "coming soon" tiles. Strongest commercial framing — "here's what's open, visit it."

What changes No separate "hero + rooms" sections. Hero collapses into the featured room. The remaining rooms appear in a 3-up "still being built" row at smaller scale.

04.
Photographic

A warm interior "photograph", polaroid rooms.

Hero is a full-bleed warm scene — layered gradients standing in for a real lifestyle photograph (terracotta + moss + warm umber). Nav goes light over the dark image. Rooms become slightly-rotated polaroid cards with handwritten room stamps in the corner. Once real photography exists, this becomes the most "magazine-cover" of the set.

What changes Hero is image-led, not type-led. Rooms aren't colored blocks — they're polaroid-style cards with a photo+caption shape. Implies real product/lifestyle photography eventually.

05.
Asymmetric mixed

Visual hierarchy = open vs building.

The "open" room is huge, the "building" rooms are small horizontal cards stacked on the right. The asymmetry IS the message — Notes is what's open today, period. Kitchen/Studio/Field listed at lower visual weight with colored left borders.

What changes Mixed tile sizes in a single asymmetric grid. Notes gets a big square tile (left); Kitchen/Studio/Field get three thin horizontal cards stacked (right). The page composition itself signals which room is open.
