Soothemade · v4 photographic · premium palette pass

Four premium palettes on the photographic shape.

You loved v4 (the warm interior photograph + polaroid rooms). These four push the colors toward more refined territory — deep jewels, restrained monochrome, pale-and-statement, editorial sage. Same shape; richer mood.

Each palette pulls from a different premium reference: gallery-private (Atelier), apothecary-restraint (Aesop), pale-luxe (bone + claret), editorial-sage (architectural-digest). All still inside Soothemade's warm-vintage type-and-voice. The four polaroid colors per palette are picked as a tight family, not a rainbow.

01.
Atelier · deep jewels on putty

A private gallery. Deep, lit, considered.

Warm putty paper instead of bright cream. The hero is a darker layered glow — burnt sienna meets pine meets warm midnight. Polaroid rooms in a tight set of deep jewel tones: oxblood (Notes), bronze (Kitchen), pine (Studio), warm-black (Field). Accent is burnt sienna — a deeper, more pigment-y terracotta.

02.
Apothecary · warm-black + amber

Most restrained of the four. Single accent.

Aesop-coded. The hero is a near-black warm gradient with subtle amber light spill. Polaroid rooms are all deep warm-blacks — they look identical at a glance, then reveal subtle undertone shifts (red-brown, bronze, green-ink, true black). Brass / amber is the only color anywhere on the page. The most monochromatic of the four.

03.
Bone & burgundy

Pale + one deep statement.

Pale parchment paper. The hero is a saturated deep burgundy / claret. Only the live (Notes) polaroid carries the claret — the other three are tonal cream/bone variants (warm bone, cool bone, putty). The hierarchy is encoded in saturation: open reads in color, building reads in neutral. Most directly says "one room open" through visual weight alone.

04.
Editorial sage + brass

Cereal magazine / architectural digest.

Deep pine hero with brass light. Polaroid rooms in the sage-and-brass family: pine (Notes), brass (Kitchen), moss (Studio), ink-green (Field). The most "green" of the four — extends Notes' existing dark forest theme into the umbrella with a more luxurious brass accent. Architectural editorial mood.
