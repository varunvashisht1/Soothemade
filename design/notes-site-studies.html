05 — Handwritten Letter

The "opening an envelope" direction

Single column. Lined paper. The whole homepage reads like a letter from Maya. Highest brand intimacy, highest founder-story signal. Trade-off: not a shop layout — products need a sub-page.

Soothemade Notes Letter № 1 · Spring 2026

Dear reader —

I made this shop on a Tuesday, because I'd bought my fourth productivity planner in a row and finally realized none of them were made for someone with a small kid.

What you'll find here is not a system. It's a small set of printables, planners, and cards for the slow, unphotographed parts of new parenthood — the 3 a.m. parts, the doorbell parts, the "I should be fine by now" parts.

Nothing here will fix you. (Nothing needs to.) But a few of these might make Tuesday easier.

— Maya

The "old broadsheet" direction

Newspaper masthead, multi-column body, double-rule borders, classified-section product grid. Distinctive and confident — reads as an essayist's shop more than a printables shop. Trade-off: dense, harder for mobile.

Editor's note

Why a small shop, made slowly, in a loud year.

We started this because the planners we kept buying weren't built for the version of us holding a baby at three in the morning. So we made our own.

— Maya, editor

This week's feature

A planner with three list slots. Not ten.

The first time I bought a productivity planner with twelve list slots a day, I was eight months pregnant. The second time, my baby was four weeks old. The third time, I was crying in a grocery aisle because I'd left my actual to-do list at home. The Slow Motherhood Planner is what came out of all of that. Four undated weeks. Three list slots a day. Reflections that don't ask if you "crushed" the week.

08 — Pinterest-Native Masonry

The "image-first, save-button" direction

Native to Pinterest's grammar. Variable-height image cards, save buttons, search-bar nav. Highest probability of converting Pinterest traffic. Trade-off: lower-prestige feel; more "Etsy storefront" than "studio."

10 — Indie Zine

The "small-press, handmade" direction

Mixed type, slight rotation, page numbers in the margins, a marquee strip, stamped corners. Most distinctive personality in the set. Highest "made by a real person" signal. Trade-off: polarizing — some readers find this charming, others find it busy.
