Soothemade · design studies

Three directions for the umbrella site.

Same content, same colors, same typefaces. Three different ways of arranging the page. Open each, scroll, react. Then point at what's working and what isn't.

What's shared across all three: the daylight palette (cream, moss, terracotta), the type stack (Fraunces, Inter, Caveat), the locked tagline "made for the parts of life no one is photographing," and the four rooms (Notes open, Kitchen / Studio / Field in the workshop). What changes: layout, density, voice register, what the page feels like when you land on it.

01.
Editorial broadsheet

A literary magazine. Front page.

Masthead with the studio name as a wordmark. Asymmetric two-column hero. Drop caps. A pull-quote inset. The rooms as a contents table, not as cards. Letters laid out as three newspaper teasers separated by vertical rules.

Feels like: opening an indie print journal. Text-dense, structured, anchored by the masthead.

02.
A letter

One narrow column. Read like correspondence.

The whole homepage reads like an opening letter from the studio. Return address top-right. Dear stranger. The rooms appear inline as a list inside the body of the letter. Signed off in Caveat. Two postscripts — one is the newsletter form. A second sheet at the bottom holds the recent letters.

Feels like: receiving a handwritten note. Intimate, single-column, conversational.

03.
Field guide

A small monograph. Numbered sections.

Title plate with a Caveat wordmark and a "field guide to a small studio" subtitle. A contents page (I–IV). Each section starts with a roman numeral in italic terracotta. The rooms as an archival catalog. The letters as catalog entries (Nº 01, Nº 02, Nº 03). Sprig dingbats between sections.

Feels like: a thin printed booklet from a museum shop. Left-aligned, structured, quiet.
