# Blog SERP Teardown — Day 1 Findings

> Method: WebSearch on 8 high-priority topics drawn from the top-tier product matrix candidates. For each, evaluated SERP weakness (thin content, listicle-heavy, ad-heavy, outdated, missing depth) and identified the rank-able angle our pillar post would attack.

## SERP weakness scoring rubric

For each topic, the top 10 results are scored:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Easy (3)** | Top results are thin listicles, no depth, no schema, no original framework |
| **Medium (2)** | Some quality content, but missing one major angle we can own |
| **Hard (1)** | Top results are authoritative, deep, well-linked. Don't attack head-on. |

## Per-topic findings

### Topic 1 — Sensory play by age (matches C35 — sensory play cards)

- **SERP weakness:** **Easy.** Top results are listicle blogs (Little Learning Club, Inspire My Play, My Bored Toddler). No comprehensive age-grid resource. Pinterest +1070% means demand far exceeds quality supply.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "The Complete Sensory Play Activity Guide by Age (0–3y, 80+ activities)." Includes per-age developmental rationale, materials list, safety notes per age. Internal-links the sensory play activity cards product.
- **Sub-queries this post can cluster:** sensory play for 6 month old, sensory play for 1 year old, sensory bin ideas toddler, sensory activities for babies at home, fine motor skill activities for toddlers, sensory play with food, edible sensory play safe, sensory toys vs activities
- **Confidence: HIGH**

### Topic 2 — Visitor boundaries after baby (matches C17 — "saying no" script cards)

- **SERP weakness:** **Easy-Medium.** Top results: Baby Tula, Mother and Baby, Postpartum Brain — all listicles. None provide actual word-for-word scripts. Big gap.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "The Postpartum Visitor Script Library: 25 things to say (and 10 things to NEVER feel guilty about)." Scripts for hospital, first week, first month, opinionated relatives, in-laws, advice-givers, etc. Internal-links the "saying no" card pack.
- **Sub-queries:** how to tell family no visitors, how to ask in-laws not to visit, saying no to mother in law baby, postpartum visitor boundaries, how to limit hospital visitors, hospital visitor restriction script, no visitors after birth message
- **Confidence: HIGH**

### Topic 3 — C-section recovery week-by-week (matches C15 — c-section recovery planner)

- **SERP weakness:** **Medium.** Top results include some quality healthcare content (Belly Bandit, Cradlewise) but they're all generic. Nobody owns the "planner / phased self-care template" angle.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "C-Section Recovery, Week by Week: What Your Body Is Doing + What to Do About It." Combines the medical timeline (with disclaimer) + emotional milestones + a per-week self-care checklist. Internal-links the 5-week planner.
- **Sub-queries:** c section recovery week 1, c section recovery week 2, c section recovery exercises, c section scar care, c section emotional recovery, when can I drive after c section, what to wear after c section, c section breastfeeding positions
- **YMYL risk:** Medium. Stay descriptive ("here's the general timeline doctors discuss"), never prescriptive. Disclaimer prominent.
- **Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH**

### Topic 4 — Slow motherhood / intentional parenting (matches C45 — slow motherhood planner)

- **SERP weakness:** **Easy.** Pinterest trend +310%. Top results are mostly opinion-pieces (Mom On Purpose, PureWow, Raising Slow). NO ranked resource owns the "planner / practice / framework" angle.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "Slow Motherhood Isn't a Luxury: How to Make It Work When You're Drowning." Reframe slow motherhood from privileged-mom-cliché into practical small-actions framework. Internal-links the weekly planner.
- **Sub-queries:** slow motherhood ideas, intentional parenting daily, slow living mom, present parenting, less busy parenting, gentle parenting routine, weekly mom planner intentional
- **Confidence: HIGH (trend tailwind)**

### Topic 5 — How to support new mom postpartum (matches C18 — partner postpartum guide)

- **SERP weakness:** **Medium.** Decent quality content here (Boram, Matrescence, Mount Carmel) but ALL of it is written for the mom audience telling her how she wishes she were supported — there's almost nothing written *to the partner directly*.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "A Letter to the Partner of a New Mom: 30 Things You Can Actually Do This Week (no toxic positivity)." Tone: written to the partner, not about him/them. Practical, concrete, ordered by sleep-recovery priority. Internal-links the Partner Postpartum Guide product.
- **Sub-queries:** how to help wife after birth, what to do for partner postpartum, dad after baby, what to do for new mom, supporting partner pregnancy, postpartum partner depression
- **Confidence: HIGH**

### Topic 6 — Screen-free summer for kids (matches C36 — screen-free challenge cards)

- **SERP weakness:** **Medium.** Top results are giant listicles ("60 screen-free activities," "100 amazing screen-free…"). They cover quantity but no one provides a structured 4–8 week challenge with daily prompts.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "The 30-Day Screen-Free Summer Challenge (with printable card deck)." Differentiates by being a structured challenge, not a list. Strong product-pull. Seasonal surge May-Aug.
- **Sub-queries:** screen free summer ideas, no phone summer challenge, screen detox kids, summer activities no screens, screen free week, screen time alternative toddler
- **Confidence: HIGH**

### Topic 7 — Return to work after maternity leave (matches C26 — back-to-work planner)

- **SERP weakness:** **Medium.** Strong commercial-intent SERP (Mom Project, Bobbie, Peacock Parent) — these are quality content. We can't beat them on "checklist" but CAN beat them on "industry-specific" and "emotional + practical combined."
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "Maternity Leave Is Ending and You're Not Ready: The Real Return-to-Work Plan (Not Just a Checklist)." Lean into emotional preparation as the differentiator. Industry-specific variations covered. Strong product CTA.
- **Sub-queries:** back to work after maternity leave anxiety, returning to work after baby 3 months, pumping at work back to work, daycare drop off first day work, working mom guilt, maternity leave ending feelings
- **Confidence: MEDIUM** (more competitive but high-value buyer)

### Topic 8 — Positive discipline phrases (matches C38 — positive-discipline parent cards)

- **SERP weakness:** **Easy-Medium.** Listicle-heavy. Few resources provide the "card-deck friendly format" — short phrases, in-the-moment language.
- **Our pillar-post angle:** "60 Positive Discipline Phrases Every Parent Wishes They Had Memorized (Free Printable Card Deck)." Provides a free 10-card freebie as lead magnet → upsell to 60-card full deck.
- **Sub-queries:** what to say instead of no, gentle parenting phrases toddler, positive discipline examples 2 year old, what to say to stop hitting, calm parent script, tantrum response phrases
- **Confidence: HIGH**

## Pillar-post writing brief (applies to all)

- Length: 2500–4500 words
- Schema: Article + HowTo (or List where applicable)
- Internal links: 2–4 to matching products + 2 cross-links to other pillar posts
- External links: only to non-competing authoritative sources (e.g., AAP, ACOG for medical), not other Etsy sellers
- Lead magnet CTA placed at: 25% scroll, 75% scroll, end
- Author byline matches brand persona (see `brand/persona.md`)
- Medical disclaimer footer on every YMYL-adjacent post

## Topics deprioritized (the kill list for pillar posts)

- **Sleep training methods** — YMYL bear-trap, controversial, churn-heavy comments
- **Feeding schedules with timing prescriptions** — YMYL, pediatrician pushback
- **Pregnancy week-by-week generic** — saturated by What to Expect, BabyCenter, Mother.ly, can't compete
- **Generic newborn schedule** — saturated, no clear ranking angle
- **Generic baby gift guides** — affiliate-saturated, low product-pull
- **Names lists / meaning lists** — purely traffic, low product-relevance
