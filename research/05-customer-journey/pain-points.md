# Customer Journey — Pain Points (Day 1)

> Synthesis from: (1) Pinterest 2026 Parenting Report, (2) WebSearch results across "what no one tells you about postpartum", "things new parents wish they knew", "new parent overwhelm survival", (3) Etsy buyer-review themes inferred from product feature lists, (4) Claude's training knowledge of major parenting forums (Reddit baby subs, BabyCenter, What to Expect community).
>
> Reddit direct scraping was blocked. Where I cite "Reddit phrases," I'm working from training-knowledge synthesis. Future session can validate with eRank-tier tools or user-supplied data.

## Phase 1 — Trying to conceive (pre-pregnancy)

**Emotional states:** hope, anxiety, monthly disappointment, fertility-tracking obsession, secrecy

**Top searches/queries:**
- "fertile window calculator app vs printable"
- "ovulation tracking journal"
- "TTC self-care"
- "secondary infertility support"

**Things they pay for:**
- Fertility / cycle tracking journals
- TTC affirmation cards
- IVF cycle planner

**Wish-existed gaps:**
- Discreet fertility journal (doesn't scream "trying to conceive" to a casual office observer)
- IVF cycle planner with medication tracker + side-effect log + emotional journal
- Miscarriage memorial keepsake (small market, real demand, almost no sellers)

## Phase 2 — First trimester (weeks 1–13)

**Emotional states:** disbelief, exhaustion, nausea, terror, secrecy, "is this normal?" anxiety

**Top searches/queries:**
- "first trimester symptoms"
- "what to do in first trimester"
- "when to tell people pregnant"
- "morning sickness remedies"
- "first prenatal appointment what to ask"

**Things they pay for:**
- Pregnancy planner with first-trimester checklist
- Symptom + appointment tracker

**Wish-existed gaps:**
- "Permission list" / what to drop (Mother.ly cited this)
- Geriatric pregnancy (35+) specific planner
- High-risk pregnancy planner with extra appointment tracking
- Pregnancy after loss / "rainbow baby" planner

**Direct pain-phrase candidates** (paraphrased from training-knowledge of forum discussions):
- "I'm so tired I can't function at work but I can't tell anyone yet"
- "Is this normal? Should I call the doctor?"
- "I forgot to ask my OB about [thing] — wish I had a list"

## Phase 5 — Birth + hospital stay

**Emotional states:** overwhelm, pain, joy, exhaustion, partner-coordination

**Top searches/queries:** (less product-search-y, more in-the-moment)
- "what to do during contractions"
- "hospital recovery"

**Things they pay for:** mostly in advance (birth plan, hospital bag prep)

**Wish-existed gaps:** partner cheat-sheet — "what to do for her during labor"

## Phase 6 — Fourth trimester (weeks 0–12 postpartum)

**This is the highest-emotional-intensity, highest-willingness-to-pay phase.** Buyers here are desperate and exhausted.

**Emotional states:** exhaustion, overwhelm, hormonal crash, identity loss, isolation, "is the baby okay?" hypervigilance, mom guilt, breastfeeding pain (if BF), bleeding/healing pain, partner relationship strain

**Top searches/queries:**
- "fourth trimester survival"
- "postpartum recovery week by week"
- "breastfeeding latch help"
- "is this postpartum depression"
- "newborn schedule"
- "baby sleeps all day awake all night"
- "what to eat while breastfeeding"
- "postpartum visitor boundaries"

**Things they pay for:**
- Postpartum recovery planner
- Affirmation cards
- Visitor boundary scripts
- Postpartum meal plan
- Breastfeeding tracker

**Wish-existed gaps:**
- C-section recovery week-by-week (5–6 week phased: pain, mobility, scar care, returning activity)
- Postpartum anxiety vs PPD self-screening worksheet
- Partner's-companion postpartum guide ("what to do for the new mom")
- "Saying no" script cards (in-laws, visitors, advice-givers)
- Pumping + work return prep
- Postpartum hair loss / body change journal

**Direct pain-phrase candidates** (paraphrased):
- "I love my baby but I miss my old life"
- "Why didn't anyone tell me about [hair loss / postpartum rage / lochia]"
- "I don't recognize my body or my mind"
- "Everyone is asking me about the baby — no one asks how I am"
- "I haven't slept more than 2 hours in 6 weeks"
- "My partner doesn't get it"

## Phase 10 — Toddler (18–36 months)

**Emotional states:** boundary-testing exhaustion, communication breakthroughs, sleep-disruption fatigue, "is this normal behavior", potty-training panic

**Top searches/queries:**
- "toddler routine chart" (Pinterest +575%)
- "potty training in 3 days"
- "tantrums age 2"
- "sensory play ideas" (Pinterest +1070%)
- "screen time toddler"
- "positive discipline" (Pinterest +295%)

**Things they pay for:**
- Visual routine cards
- Chore charts
- Potty training tracker
- Sensory activity cards
- Positive discipline scripts

**Wish-existed gaps:**
- **HUGE** — sensory play card decks by age (mismatch between Pinterest demand +1070% and Etsy supply)
- Screen-free week / month challenges
- "Tantrum decoder" — what's behind the tantrum + parent response scripts
- Positive-discipline phrase cards (in-the-moment language for the parent, not the kid)
- Travel activity packets (road trip / flight)
- Sleep-disruption journal (record when/why, identify patterns — non-prescriptive)

## Cross-phase pain themes (the "always-on" pains)

1. **Identity loss** — "I don't know who I am anymore" — applies from late pregnancy through year 3
2. **Overwhelm + decision fatigue** — too many choices, too much advice
3. **Sleep deprivation** — eternal
4. **Mom guilt** — perpetual
5. **Partner-coordination friction** — "we're not on the same page"
6. **Isolation** — loneliness despite being constantly with a small human
7. **Information overload** — fire-hose of advice from every direction
8. **Comparison anxiety** — Instagram, in-laws, other moms

## Buyer segments (sorted by willingness-to-pay)

| Segment | Income / WTP | Best for us? |
|---|---|---|
| **Working mom (corporate)** | High | ✅ Yes — pays for time-savers; back-to-work planner is killer |
| **NICU / preemie parents** | Mid-high (desperate) | ✅ Yes — small but high WTP, low competition |
| **Twin / multiples parents** | Mid-high (desperate) | ✅ Yes — same logic |
| **High-risk / IVF / advanced age** | Mid-high | ✅ Yes — same logic |
| **Faith-based mom** | Mid | ✅ Maybe — sub-segment with strong loyalty; not our default lane |
| **First-time mom (general)** | Mid | ✅ Yes — broad market, more competition |
| **Multi-kid mom** | Mid | ✅ Yes — under-served, less competition than first-time-mom |
| **Stay-at-home mom** | Lower (price-sensitive) | ⚠️ Mixed — lower price tolerance |
| **Teen / very young mom** | Lower (price-sensitive) | ⚠️ Skip — different aesthetic, lower WTP |
