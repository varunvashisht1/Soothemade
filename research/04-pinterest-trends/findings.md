# Pinterest Trends — Day 1 Findings

> Source: Pinterest's 2026 Parenting Trend Report (their first-ever), summarized via multiple secondary articles. Numbers below are year-over-year search-growth percentages reported by Pinterest.

## The macro theme

**"Raising screen-smart kids who seek real-world adventure."** This shapes everything. Parents on Pinterest in 2026 are searching for offline, hands-on, experience-rich, slower content. This is the *aesthetic and value frame* we should align all our products with.

## Travel/experience cluster (rising)

Road-trip-coded searches surging:
- small car camping
- travel photobook
- road trip car set up
- free road trip printables

**Opportunity:** "Toddler road trip activity packet" (printable mazes, scavenger hunts, sticker books) — under-served on Etsy, surging on Pinterest.

## Aesthetic shift for 2026

The dominant Pinterest aesthetic is moving from:
- **Out:** Sterile minimalist, all-white, Goodnotes-tablet-focused
- **In:** Warm vintage, 70s/90s textures, earthy palettes, kraft paper, hand-lettered, "lived-in cozy"

**Implication for our brand:** the saturated digital-planner aesthetic (black-on-white minimalist) is going out of style. We can ride the trend by leaning vintage-warm: kraft paper backgrounds, slightly aged textures, hand-lettered titles, muted earth palette.

This is *defensible differentiation* — most established sellers won't retool their entire catalog to match a new aesthetic.

## Pin format recipe

Based on what we know about Pinterest's algorithm + 2026 trends:

| Format | When to use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Static text-on-image pin** | Pillar blog posts | 1000x1500, 2:3 ratio |
| **Carousel pin** | Listing showcases ("5 reasons this planner works") | New format, currently boosted |
| **Idea pin (video)** | Top 10–15 hero products | Use ElevenLabs voiceover; 15–30s |
| **Infographic pin** | Blog-post topics like "first trimester checklist" | High save rate, drives blog traffic |

Per product, plan: 5 static + 1 video pin = 6 pins per product = ~180 pins across 30 products.
