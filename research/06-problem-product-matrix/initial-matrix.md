## Scoring rubric

Each opportunity scored 1–10 on five dimensions:

- **Demand:** How many people want this? (Reddit/forum signal + Pinterest trend + Etsy listing-density inverse)
- **Competition:** Lower is better. 1 = saturated, 10 = wide open
- **Buildability:** Can we produce this with HTML/CSS + Canva + ElevenLabs only? 10 = yes, easy
- **Margin:** Combination of price ceiling + repeat-purchase likelihood
- **YMYL safety:** 10 = no medical/safety advice required, 1 = high risk

**Composite** = (Demand × 2) + Competition + (Buildability × 1.5) + Margin + (YMYL safety × 1.5), max ~70.

## Notes on selection logic

- **Pinterest 2026 trends were heavily weighted** — they're the strongest leading-indicator signal we have for what will sell over the next 12 months.
- **Saturated categories (pregnancy planner, newborn tracker, baby shower games) are intentionally absent from the top tier** — competition crushes new shops there.
- **Specialty/niche segments (twin, NICU, IVF, c-section) scored well** — desperate buyers + low competition + emotional spend.
- **YMYL-adjacent items (sleep training, feeding schedules, GD meal plans) were penalized but not eliminated** — they're still buildable as journals/trackers without medical advice.
