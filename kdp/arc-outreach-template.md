# ARC Reader Outreach

ARC = Advance Reader Copy. The goal: 15-25 honest reviews on each book within
the first 30 days of launch, because Kindle's algorithm rewards review velocity
heavily in that window.

## Who to ask

Tier 1 — high-trust, high-conversion (highest priority):
- Postpartum doulas you know personally or via professional networks
- IBCLCs (lactation consultants) — they'll review B03, B10, B12
- Pediatric occupational therapists — for B04 (Big Feelings)
- Therapists specializing in perinatal mental health — for B06 (Birth Is Scary)
- Geriatric social workers — for B11, B12 (eldercare)
- Friends who have had a baby in the last 3 years

Tier 2 — broader reach:
- Mom-bloggers in the slow-living / minimalist parenting niche
- Instagram accounts focused on postpartum recovery
- Substack writers in the parenting / care-work space
- TikTok creators who do "books I loved" review videos (note: most won't reply,
  but the ones who do drive disproportionate sales)

Tier 3 — skip until you have data:
- BookTok influencers (out of niche)
- Paid review services (Kindle bans these — never use)
- Random Goodreads users (low conversion, slow turnaround)

## How many copies to give away

Plan for ~30 ARC giveaways per book. Expectations:
- ~10-15 will actually read it
- ~5-8 will leave a review on Amazon
- ~3-5 will leave a review on Goodreads
- ~1-2 will share on social

That's the realistic conversion. Plan accordingly.

## Email template — Tier 1 (professional contacts)

Subject: `A small ask — would you read a draft?`

```
Hi [Name],

I'm Maya at Soothemade — we've talked about [the meal train / the Wednesday session / the eldercare workshop, etc., make it specific].

I've just finished writing [BOOK TITLE], a [one-sentence description from the KDP description hook]. It launches on Kindle next week.

Would you be willing to read it and, if it's any good, leave an honest review? I'd send you a free copy ahead of launch — Kindle file or PDF, whichever you prefer.

No pressure if the timing's wrong, and definitely no pressure to leave a positive review if the book doesn't land for you. I'm asking because I trust your eye on this material, and Kindle's algorithm gives a small book like this a chance only if real readers show up in the first few weeks.

Either way — thank you for the work you do.

Maya
soothemade.com
```

## Email template — Tier 2 (mom bloggers / Instagram)

Subject: `Free ARC of a slow-motherhood book — interested?`

```
Hi [Name],

I've been a quiet reader of your work for [duration] — [one specific thing you've loved, make it specific not generic].

I just finished writing a short book called [TITLE]. It's a [one-sentence pitch]. It launches on Kindle [date].

I'm sending ARC copies to writers I respect in the slow-living-parenthood corner of the internet. Would you be open to a copy? No requirement to review or share — but if it lands for you and you do want to mention it, that would matter.

ePub or PDF — whichever works for your reading setup.

Thank you for considering,
Maya
soothemade.com
```

## What NOT to do

- Don't ask for "5-star reviews." Asking for positive reviews violates
  Amazon's Community Guidelines and can get reviews removed + your account
  flagged.
- Don't send the same email to 50 people at once with BCC. Personalize each
  one. The opening line should reference a specific thing about them.
- Don't follow up more than once. If they don't respond, they're not interested
  (or the email got buried). Either way, move on.
- Don't offer payment, gift cards, or services in exchange for a review.
- Don't review your own book under a different account. Amazon catches this
  and bans permanently.

## Tracking

Use `kdp/launch-tracker.csv` to log:
- Who you contacted (one row per outreach if you want to track at that
  granularity — or just log in your CRM/notes)
- Who said yes
- Who actually reviewed
- Where the review landed (Amazon / Goodreads / Instagram / etc.)
