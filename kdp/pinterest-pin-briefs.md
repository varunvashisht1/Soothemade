# Pinterest Pin Briefs — Hero Books

Pinterest is Soothemade's strongest channel. Sage palette + warm-vintage
aesthetic + emotional-specific copy = the algorithm rewards exactly this.

The three hero books for the Pinterest push are **B01, B07, B11** — chosen for:
- **B01:** Most concrete (30 named scripts = 30 pin opportunities)
- **B07:** Most quotable / shareable (manifesto-shaped)
- **B11:** Longest shelf-life (eldercare buyers search year-round, not seasonally)

Five pin concepts per book = 15 base pins. Generate 3-5 variants of each in
Canva. End-state: 45-75 pins per book queued up.

---

## Visual rules (all 15 pins)

- **Palette:** Brand sage (`#EAEEE2` background, `#8C9B7A` primary, `#5C5347`
  ink-soft) — same as the book covers
- **Aspect ratio:** 1000×1500 (Pinterest's optimal vertical)
- **Typography:** Caveat for the hook quote (large), Fraunces italic for
  context, Inter small-caps for credits
- **Visual elements:** soft-focus photography or single sage botanical sprig.
  No people. No babies' faces. No clip art.
- **End sticker:** small Soothemade wordmark + book title at the bottom in
  Inter small-caps, ~3% of the pin height
- **No price.** Pinterest hates direct sales pitches. The pin is a feeling, the
  click-through is to the book.

## Pinterest workflow

1. **Set up Pinterest Business account.** Verify the domain
   `notes.soothemade.com` (Pinterest needs this for analytics access).
2. **Create 3 boards:**
   - "For New Parents" (B01, B03, B04, B07, B10, B08 pins)
   - "Pregnancy + Birth" (B05, B06, B09 pins)
   - "Eldercare + the Sandwich Years" (B02, B11, B12 pins)
3. **Pin schedule:** 10 pins/day for 60 days, mixing the 45-75 you've made
   across the books. Pinterest's Tailwind scheduler is the standard tool
   (~$13/month).
4. **Track:** Pin analytics in Pinterest dashboard. Watch for any pin that
   passes 1000 impressions in week 1 — that's the breakout candidate. Make
   10 more variants of that pin.
5. **Compounding window:** 3-6 months. Don't expect anything in week 1-2.

## What NOT to do

- Don't post the book cover as the pin. Pinterest users don't save covers;
  they save quotes and aesthetic photography.
- Don't put a price on the pin. Pinterest is discovery, not e-commerce.
- Don't use Canva templates labeled "Pinterest pin template." Pinterest
  algorithmically demotes content that matches obvious template patterns.
  Customize the Soothemade brand into each pin.
- Don't pin the same image to multiple boards on the same day. Counts as spam.
- Don't follow more than 30-50 accounts in a day. Triggers spam flag.
