# P52 — The Birth Anxiety Workbook

A 40-page workbook for the fear of giving birth. Validates the fear, names the specific sub-fears, gives tools that are not platitudes, scripts for telling the team you are scared, and the routing to perinatal mental-health care when the anxiety is bigger than the workbook can hold.

## Why this exists

Birth anxiety is widespread and underdiscussed. Pregnant people are routinely told to "trust your body" or "women have done this for thousands of years," which are dismissive of a real fear about a real event with real risks. This workbook treats the fear seriously, names the specific sub-fears, and offers practical tools instead of platitudes.

## YMYL care

- YMYL careful. Birth is medical territory.
- The workbook does NOT predict outcomes.
- It does NOT recommend specific birth methods.
- It does NOT name medications used in labor.
- Perinatal mental-health crisis lines are listed.
- A clear "if your anxiety is bigger than this workbook, see a perinatal therapist" page is included.

## Scope

Approximately 40 pages:

- Cover, 3 front-matter pages
- "Birth IS scary" validating page
- The fear inventory page (multiple fears, named)
- 2-page workbook for each of 6 major fears:
  - Pain
  - Loss of control
  - Complications (mom)
  - Complications (baby)
  - Interventions (c-section, tearing, episiotomy, etc.)
  - The unknown
- A "what would help me specifically" page
- Scripts for telling the team you are scared
- The night-before-labor page
- The "I am not okay" perinatal mental-health routing page
- Crisis lines
- A final letter
