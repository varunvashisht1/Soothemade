You are afraid of giving birth.

You have been told to "trust your body." You have been told "women have done this for thousands of years." You have been told that the fear is irrational. None of those have helped, because the fear is not irrational. Birth is a real event with real risks and real intensity. The fear is information about a body about to do something enormous.

This workbook treats the fear seriously. It names what you are actually afraid of, not "the birth" in the abstract. It gives tools that are not platitudes. It gives you scripts for telling the medical team you are scared. It routes you to perinatal mental-health care when the anxiety is bigger than the workbook can hold.

## What's inside

Forty pages, fillable:

- **A note before you start.** Why fear is not weakness.
- **"Birth IS scary."** A validating page.
- **The fear inventory.** Naming the specific fears.
- **Six 2-page fear workbooks:**
  - Pain
  - Loss of control
  - Complications for you
  - Complications for the baby
  - Interventions you might not want
  - The unknown
- **"What would help me, specifically" page.**
- **Scripts for telling the team you are scared.**
- **The night-before-labor page.**
- **The "I am not okay" page.** With crisis lines.
- **A final letter.**
