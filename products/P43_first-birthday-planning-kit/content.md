# A First Birthday Planning Kit

*Twenty pages. Six weeks of planning. A day, not an event.*

---

## A note before you start

The first birthday is a day.

It is not a Pinterest production. It is not a backyard wedding. It is not a brand activation. It is the kid turning one, with cake on their face and a few people who love them in the room.

This kit is the version of planning that respects that. You will not need a vendor. You will not need a "thematic palette." You will not need a balloon arch unless you really want one.

The 6-week countdown is the work. After the countdown, you have a day. The day will be fine. The kid will not remember it. Your photos will.

*With care, Maya*

> *This is a planning tool. Allergies and dietary restrictions in your guest list are between you and the families involved.*

---

## You do not have to make this huge

This is the page to come back to when you feel the pressure to upgrade.

```
PERMISSION SLIPS

You are allowed to have 6 guests, not 30.
You are allowed to skip the venue and do it at home.
You are allowed to have a store-bought cake.
You are allowed to skip the smash cake.
You are allowed to skip the balloon arch.
You are allowed to skip a theme entirely.
You are allowed to do it on a different weekend than the actual birthday.
You are allowed to do it in pajamas in the morning.
You are allowed to keep it to two hours.
You are allowed to call it done.

The kid will not remember any of it. You will.
The kit's job is to make sure the day feels good to YOU,
not to perform well on someone else's feed.
```

## Theme ideas (low-effort, brand-aligned)

You do not need a theme. If you want one, here are five that do not require expert decorating.

```
THEME 1: "Just turning one"
   Decor: A small banner. Candle. Cake.
   Time investment: 30 minutes
   Best for: Small home celebration

THEME 2: "Garden / botanical"
   Decor: Eucalyptus, small potted plants, sage table runner
   Time investment: 2 hours of running around to a florist or hardware
   store + plant nursery
   Best for: Spring or summer birthdays, outdoor setting

THEME 3: "Sage + cream + cake"
   Decor: A sage tablecloth, cream napkins, a single cake with one
   candle. Eucalyptus.
   Time investment: 1 hour to set up
   Best for: Anyone who likes the Soothemade aesthetic and does not
   want to invent

THEME 4: "Books + babies"
   Decor: A pile of children's books arranged as decor, plus a guestbook
   that is actually a children's book each guest signs
   Time investment: 1 hour
   Best for: Households that already have many books and want a
   meaningful keepsake

THEME 5: "Coffee + pastries"
   Decor: A morning party, pastries from a local bakery, coffee for
   adults, a small cake for the kid
   Time investment: A grocery run
   Best for: Morning birthdays, low-key gatherings

THE ONE I AM DOING:                ____________________

ANYTHING I AM ADDING / CHANGING:
_______________________________________________
```

## Food + cake

A first-birthday food spread does not have to be elaborate.

```
THE EASY-WIN FORMAT

   One main thing (a sandwich tray, a pasta dish, a few pizzas)
   Two sides (a fruit platter, a vegetable platter)
   One sweet thing besides the cake (cookies, small pastries)
   One drink other than water (lemonade, coffee, tea)
```

## Smash cake, or not

A decision page.

```
WHAT IS A SMASH CAKE

   A small individual cake (often a 4-inch round) given to the baby
   to literally smash with their hands. Often photographed.

WHY YOU MIGHT DO IT

   ☐ It is a Pinterest-classic photo set
   ☐ The kid loves cake and food
   ☐ You want a specific memory

WHY YOU MIGHT NOT DO IT

   ☐ It is messy and the kid may hate it
   ☐ Babies sometimes do not actually like the experience of frosting
   ☐ You do not want sugar yet
   ☐ It is one more thing to organize

VARIATIONS IF YOU WANT THE PHOTO BUT NOT THE MESS

   ☐ A "smash bagel" or "smash muffin" instead (less sugar, lower mess)
   ☐ A smash fruit (a small bowl of berries to mash)
   ☐ Skip the smash, just give the baby a slice of the main cake

MY DECISION:

   ○ Yes, traditional smash cake
   ○ Yes, a variation (which: ____________________)
   ○ No smash, just regular cake at the party
   ○ No cake involved, just photos with candles and the family

THE PHOTO SETUP IF I AM DOING IT:

   ☐ Plain backdrop (sheet, paper, wall)
   ☐ High chair or floor on a sheet
   ☐ Bib + soft outfit (something I do not mind throwing out if
     the frosting wins)
   ☐ Towel + change of clothes ready
```

## Day-of timeline

For nobody to have to ask "when is X."

```
MORNING (BEFORE GUESTS)

  ___:___  Wake up (parent + baby)
  ___:___  Get the food + space set up
  ___:___  Get yourself + baby dressed
  ___:___  Final cleanup pass
  ___:___  First guests arrive

DURING THE PARTY

  ___:___  Guests arrive (open the door, hug, point to drinks)
  ___:___  Mingling, baby plays
  ___:___  Group photo (do this early, before the cake)
  ___:___  Food served
  ___:___  Cake + singing happy birthday
  ___:___  Smash cake (if doing)
  ___:___  Cards or quote-book signing
  ___:___  Last guests leave

AFTER

  ___:___  Cleanup begins
  ___:___  Baby nap or bedtime (likely earlier than usual, party kids
           are often overstimulated)
  ___:___  Adults can sit down

A NOTE: PARTIES THAT END BY 2-2.5 HOURS WORK BETTER FOR ONE-YEAR-OLDS.
LONGER PARTIES MEAN OVERTIRED MELTDOWN AT THE END, BOTH KID + PARENT.

A SECOND NOTE: HAVE A PLAN FOR THE LAST GUEST WHO STAYS TOO LONG.
A LINE LIKE "WE ARE SO GLAD YOU CAME, BABY IS ABOUT TO HIT NAP TIME"
IS FINE.
```

## A final note

You planned a day. You showed up for the cake. You took photos. You held the kid. You ate a small slice of cake yourself.

The party is the smaller part. The year you spent learning who they are is the bigger part.

The letter you wrote, the photos you took, the quote-book your guests signed, those are the things you will pull out at five years old, at ten, at eighteen. The kid will not remember the cake. They will read the letter.

You made it through year one. That is the milestone the cake is actually for.

*With care, Maya*
