## Listing description

```
Most postpartum planners assume you walked out of the hospital.

If you had a c-section — you didn't. You had major abdominal
surgery. You can't lift more than the baby for six weeks. You
can't drive for two. You're tracking pain medication on a
schedule. The incision needs daily checking.

This is the postpartum planner built for that.

WHAT'S INSIDE — 36 pages, 5 phases

▸ WEEK 1 — The Hardest Week
   What's generally happening in your body, realistic activity
   guidance, daily care log with pain + medication + incision
   tracking, mental-health watch list, end-of-week reflection.

▸ WEEK 2 — Slightly More Functional
   Watch list for setbacks, what activities to NOT do (this is when
   most c-section moms accidentally over-do), daily log + reflection.

▸ WEEK 3 — The "I Should Be Fine By Now" Trap
   When hormones drop again, the delayed-PPD watch, why this week
   tricks you, daily log + reflection.

▸ WEEK 4 — Establishing the New Normal
   The "first day solo" prep page, what to set out at arm's reach,
   daily log + reflection.

▸ WEEKS 5–6 — Pre-Clearance
   Specific questions to ask at your 6-week appointment, prep for
   exercise return, pelvic floor PT discussion, final reflection.

▸ SUPPORTING PAGES
   - Medication tracker
   - Incision care log
   - Partner handoff page (tear out and tape up)
   - Mental-health hotline contact info

WHY THIS IS DIFFERENT

Generic postpartum planners are written for vaginal birth recovery.
C-section adds: surgical incision care, longer activity restriction,
specific watch list for blood clots / infection, different timeline
for driving and sex, different exercise return.

This planner is built around all of that.

WHO THIS IS FOR

▸ Scheduled c-section moms (start before delivery)
▸ Emergency c-section moms (start in the hospital)
▸ Partners of c-section moms (gift)
▸ Anyone supporting a c-section mom (sister, mom, friend)

YOUR FILES

▸ Instant download PDF, 36 pages, US Letter + A4
▸ Yours forever
▸ Personal use only

IMPORTANT — THIS IS NOT MEDICAL ADVICE

This planner is a general framework and tracking tool. Every c-section
recovery is individual. Always follow your surgeon's specific
instructions. If something feels wrong, call your OB.

Mental health: US Maternal Mental Health Hotline 1-833-852-6262.
Crisis: 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).

— Soothemade
```
