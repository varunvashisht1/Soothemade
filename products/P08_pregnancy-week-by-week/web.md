I kept a pregnancy journal with my first. It asked me to rate my "glow" each week on a scale of one to ten. By week twelve I'd put it in a drawer.

This is the journal I went looking for after that one. Not a quiz, not a wellness scorecard. A place to put down what happened this week, what you noticed, what your body was doing, what you were afraid of, what made you laugh in spite of everything.

## What's inside

Eighty pages. Two pages per week from week six through forty:

- **The left page** is yours: body notes, sleep, what was new, what you ate that worked or didn't, the one thing you don't want to forget.
- **The right page** is for the things outside you: appointments, partner notes, a tiny space for a sticker or a wrap-line measurement, and three lines you'd write to the baby if you wanted to. No pressure.

Plus:

- **A "before you start" page** for setting the rest of it down honestly. Why you're keeping this. Who, if anyone, you'd let read it.
- **Trimester dividers** with one quiet page each. Not a checklist.
- **A symptom reference**, in plain language. What's worth a call, what's worth a wait.
- **Three end-of-trimester reflection prompts**, one per trimester. Open-ended. Skip them if you'd rather.
- **A final page**, week forty (or forty-one, or forty-two). For the night before, whenever that night comes.

## Who this is for

You, if it's a first baby and you want a place to put the whole strange experience.

You, if it's a second or third and you barely got to journal the first because, well, the first was already there.

You, if you've had losses before and need a journal that doesn't assume forty smooth weeks.

You, if your pregnancy is high-risk, single, by donor, by IVF, by surprise, after grief, after waiting too long. All of it gets a page.

## Format

Instant-download PDF, US Letter + A4. Print the whole thing, or print one week at a time. Personal use only.
