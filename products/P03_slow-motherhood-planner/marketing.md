## Gumroad sales page

**URL slug:** `/slow-motherhood-planner`

**Product name:** *The Slow Motherhood Weekly Planner*

**Subtitle:** *A 36-page, 4-week undated planner that's allowed to be used badly.*

**Price:** $12 (PWYW with $12 minimum)

**Sales-page body:**

```
This planner has three list slots per day. Not ten.

I made it on a Tuesday when I bought my fourth productivity planner
in a row and realized I'd never actually used one. They all had
ten list slots. They all had morning routines I was supposed to
build. They all had "habit tracker" grids that I'd quietly abandon
by Thursday.

None of them were designed for a mom of small kids.

Because a mom of small kids cannot complete a 12-slot to-do list
on a normal day. She also cannot do a 5am morning routine without
sacrificing the only sleep she has. She also probably should not
be trying to "build the habit" of 30 minutes of journaling when
the only quiet 30 minutes of the day is the only nap of the day.

So I made this. It has:

▸ 3 list slots per day. Not 10.
▸ 2 daily questions. Not 20.
▸ 4 weekly spreads. Not 7 daily ones.
▸ Reflections that don't ask if you "crushed" the week.
▸ A monthly review that asks where you found 2 minutes of yourself.
▸ Permission, repeated throughout, to skip pages without guilt.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHAT'S INSIDE — 36 pages, undated

The opening: a 5-minute intention exercise (or 5 sittings of 1
minute each, the planner doesn't care).

Four weekly spreads. Each one is:
▸ A week-ahead "invitation" page (not a goal page)
▸ Seven daily pages with the same two questions:
   ▹ What's one thing for me today?
   ▹ What's one thing for the kids today?
▸ A weekly reflection that names what worked AND what you let go of

A monthly reflection that asks:
▸ What were the 3 best moments this month?
▸ What were the hardest 3?
▸ What surprised you?
▸ What's something you did this month that wasn't sustainable?
▸ Where did you say yes when you meant no?

A closing note that won't make you feel bad about the pages you
skipped.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHY UNDATED MATTERS

Dated planners shame you when you miss a day. Then a week. Then
you stop opening it.

This is undated. Start it in March, abandon it in April, pick it
up again in July. The planner won't tell. It will look exactly
the same. So will the work it's asking you to do.

The undated, no-deadline, no-shame nature of this thing is the
entire point.

WHAT THIS IS NOT

▸ A productivity system. We have enough of those.
▸ A "habit tracker." Trackers are for athletes, not exhausted moms.
▸ A goal-setting framework. You already know what you want to do.
  You can't do it because there's no time. The planner can't fix
  time.
▸ Toxic-positive. No "you've got this!" no "rise and grind." Just
  honest space.

WHO THIS IS FOR

▸ Moms 0–5 (or older — slow motherhood doesn't expire)
▸ The mom who bought 3 planners last year and didn't use any
▸ Anyone tired of the "morning routine influencer" loop
▸ The friend you'd send a slow-living book to

YOUR FILES

▸ Instant download PDF, 36 pages, US Letter + A4
▸ Yours forever in your Gumroad library
▸ Personal use only

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use it badly. That's how it works.

— Soothemade
```

**Bundle upsell:** Bundle with P02 (Saying No Scripts) for the "tough seasons of mom-life" pack — $16 instead of $19.

**After-purchase email:** Subject: *"the only page to start with"*. Body: Point them to the 5-minute intention exercise (page 3). Tell them they can do it in 1-minute pieces. End with permission to skip and come back.
