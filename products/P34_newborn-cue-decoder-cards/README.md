# P34 — Newborn Cue Decoder Cards

A 30-card deck for new parents trying to read a baby who can't talk yet. Organized into four packs: cries, sleep cues, hunger cues, overstimulation cues, with a small bonus pack of connection cues. Each card describes the cue in plain language, what it most often means, and one or two things to try.

## Why this exists

The first six weeks are largely about translation. New parents repeatedly ask, "what is this cry, what is this face, what does it mean." Books give long answers. Apps give algorithmic guesses. This deck gives a 3x5 card per cue, written in plain language. Slips into the diaper bag.

## YMYL care

- Low-YMYL. Cards describe behavioral cues, not symptoms.
- Every "what to try" is suggestive ("you might"), never prescriptive ("you must").
- A dedicated "when to call the pediatrician" card and a "when the cue isn't fixing it" page route the parent out of the deck and toward their provider when warranted.
- No medication mentions. No condition names.

## Scope

Approximately 30 pages including:

- Cover, parent note, how-to-use, "every baby is different" disclaimer
- Cries pack: 8 cards
- Sleep cues pack: 6 cards
- Hunger cues pack: 6 cards
- Overstimulation pack: 6 cards
- Connection cues pack: 4 cards (bonus)
- When the cue isn't fixing it page
- When to call the pediatrician page
- A final note
