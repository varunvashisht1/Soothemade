# A Rainbow Baby Planner

*Thirty pages for the pregnancy that is carrying two stories at the same time.*

---

## A note before you start

You are pregnant again. After.

After the loss, whichever kind it was. After the appointment where the room got quiet. After the day they sent you home with a number to call.

This pregnancy is happening in a body that remembers. The mind too. The mind is keeping a calendar against the previous calendar. The body is waiting for the day it stopped before. The phrase "I have a good feeling about this one" feels like a trap that has been set for you twice.

There is no part of this that is unreasonable.

This planner is for that experience. It is not a grief book. P20 Pregnancy Loss Gentle Companion is the grief book. This is the planner for the second story, the one running alongside.

You can use it for the whole pregnancy. You can use it for a season and then put it down. You can use it together with a regular pregnancy planner, the way two stories sit next to each other. You can put it in a drawer the day the baby comes home, or you can use it for the next pregnancy too. There is no right way.

*With care, Maya*

> *This is a planning and tracking tool, not medical advice. For specific medical questions about this pregnancy, please follow your obstetric provider's guidance. This planner does not name medications or recommend specific care, and it does not replace mental-health care for perinatal anxiety or trauma. Crisis lines are listed at the back.*

## How to use this planner

- The memorial pages are optional. You can skip them. You can keep them. You can fill them in later.
- The "anxiety in pregnancy after loss" page is worth reading early. Some patterns are useful to recognize before they are overwhelming.
- Each trimester has an emotional checkpoint page. The trimester of dread, the trimester of starting to hope, the trimester of almost.
- The "if it happens again" page is in the back. You do not have to read it. It is there because pretending the question is not there often makes the question louder. You can keep that page closed.
- Take what you want. Leave what you do not.

## Anxiety in pregnancy after loss, what is common

This page lists common patterns. It is not prescribing how to manage them. It is naming them so you can recognize them in yourself.

**Anniversary anxiety.** Around the week the previous loss happened, anxiety often spikes. Even if this pregnancy is doing fine. Even if your provider is reassuring. The body is on a calendar.

**Symptom-checking.** Many rainbow parents check for symptoms (or absence of symptoms) constantly. Every cramp. Every bathroom trip. Every change in nausea. This is the body trying to hold control where it cannot have it.

**Appointment fear.** The 24 hours before an appointment is often the hardest. The hour after is often relief, sometimes followed by guilt or numbness.

**Bonding fear.** Many rainbow parents do not allow themselves to attach to this pregnancy until late. The fear of loss is loud. This is normal. Bonding can happen at any pace. It does not predict the outcome.

**Hyperarousal.** Trouble sleeping. Easily startled. Catastrophic thoughts. These are common after pregnancy loss, and they are also signs of perinatal anxiety.

**Avoidance.** Some rainbow parents do not look at baby clothes. Do not let people throw a shower. Do not pack a hospital bag until very late. This is the body protecting itself.

**Reassurance-seeking.** Calling the doctor's office repeatedly. Buying a home doppler and using it many times a day. Asking your partner the same question many times. The reassurance does not stay.

## When fear shows up

It will. This page is what to reach for in the middle of the night, when the body is sure something is wrong.

**First, the body checks.** Are you in pain that is unusual. Is there bleeding that is heavy. Is there a change that warrants a call.

If yes, call your provider. The on-call line exists for this.

If no, the next thing is grounding.

**Grounding for the middle of the night:**

- Sit up. Put your feet on the floor. Feel the cold.
- Name five things you can see in the room.
- Name four things you can touch.
- Name three things you can hear.
- Name two things you can smell.
- Name one thing you can taste.
- Drink a glass of water.
- Wake the partner if you need to.

**A small mantra that some rainbow parents use:**

- "Right now, in this exact moment, the baby is okay."

You do not have to know what will happen tomorrow. You only have to be okay in this exact moment. Sometimes that is one minute at a time.

## Partner page

For the support person. The previous loss happened in two lives. So is this pregnancy.

The grief from before is sometimes asymmetric. Sometimes the partner who did not carry the previous baby is also carrying significant grief. Sometimes the partner is more okay than the pregnant person. Sometimes the other way. Sometimes neither of you can talk about it without it getting fractious.

```
WHAT THIS PREGNANCY IS ASKING OF THE SUPPORT PERSON

DURING APPOINTMENTS

   ☐ Be there. Most rainbow parents need a body in the room.
   ☐ Watch your person, not just the monitor.
   ☐ Ask the question your person was too scared to ask.
   ☐ Take notes. The pregnant person may not remember the
     conversation after.

AT HOME

   ☐ Do not say "this pregnancy is different" as a reassurance
     to your partner. They know it is different. The fear is
     not about logic.
   ☐ Do not say "everything will be fine." You do not know that.
     Saying it makes them feel alone with the fear.
   ☐ Do say "I am here. We are doing this together. Whichever
     way it goes, we are still us."
   ☐ Watch for the anniversary dates. Mark them on the calendar.
   ☐ Bring up the previous baby when it feels right. Not bringing
     them up at all sometimes feels like erasure.

YOUR OWN GRIEF

   This pregnancy may surface your own grief about the previous
   loss in new ways. That is real. The grief is not a competition
   between the two of you. Both of you carry it.

   If you need your own therapist, that is allowed. Many partners
   of rainbow parents see one.
```

This is the page no one wants to write down. The planner is doing it because pretending it is not in the room often makes it louder.

## Crisis lines

If thoughts of harm to yourself or to the baby are present, call.

**United States:**

- 988, Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text)
- 1-833-852-6262 (PSI), Postpartum Support International HelpLine
- 1-800-944-4773, PSI HelpLine, alternate number

**United Kingdom:**

- 116 123, Samaritans
- 0808 1640 8888, Tommy's Pregnancy Loss Helpline

**Canada:**

- 9-8-8, Suicide Crisis Helpline (call or text)
- 1-833-456-4566, Canada Suicide Prevention Service

Your country has a number even if it is not here. Search "crisis line [your country]." Save the number now, before you need it.

A note: pregnancy loss and pregnancy-after-loss are widely supported in perinatal mental-health communities. You are not alone, even when the room feels empty.

## A final letter

You are doing something hard.

You are pregnant in a body that remembers. You are showing up for appointments that cost you something. You are eating, sleeping, working, parenting (sometimes) other children, while a part of you holds your breath for nine months.

Whichever way this pregnancy ends, you did the work. The carrying. The hoping. The making of the small space for this baby that exists alongside the small space you have made for the previous one.

If this pregnancy delivers, that baby is not a replacement. They are their own person. They will know, eventually, that they have a sibling who came first. You will figure out when and how to tell them.

If this pregnancy does not deliver, you carried again. That is its own act of courage. The grief is layered. The community for this exists, and you are not the only person who has done this twice or more.

You are not crazy. You are not selfish. You are not "being too much." You are pregnant after loss. This is what it is.

Whatever you write in this planner, or do not write, is yours.

*With care, Maya*
