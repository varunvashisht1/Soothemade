## Gumroad sales page

**URL slug:** `/partner-postpartum-playbook`

**Product name:** *The Partner's Postpartum Playbook*

**Subtitle:** *A 24-page guide written TO the partner, not to her. What to do, in what order, for the first 12 weeks.*

**Price:** $9 (PWYW with $9 minimum)

**Sales-page body:**

```
Here's what most "how to support your wife after baby" guides get
wrong: they're written to her, telling her how to ask for help.

By the time she has the words to ask, she's been depleted for a
week.

This is the opposite. It's written to you — the partner. Concrete
actions. In order of priority. Week by week. No "be supportive" or
"communicate openly" — those words are useless at 3am when she's
crying in the kitchen.

Real actions. Real situations. Real scripts.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHAT'S INSIDE — 24 pages

▸ The first 48 hours — gatekeeping, the bag, the visitors, what to
  do without asking
▸ Week 1 — Survival mode in priority order: food + water, the
  feed/diaper/sleep stack, the bleeding situation, the shower, the
  visitors
▸ Week 2 — The daily check-in question that actually works, plus
  a postpartum mental-health watch with specific signals
▸ Week 3 — Why this is when partners check out, and the cost of
  doing it
▸ Weeks 4–6 — Re-entry without "giving the household back"
▸ Weeks 7–12 — Fourth trimester deeper stuff: delayed PPD, postpartum
  rage, identity grief, relationship friction
▸ Special-situation playbooks: c-section, breastfeeding, formula
  feeding, birth trauma, same-sex couples, back-to-work
▸ The conversations to have BEFORE baby (7 specific topics)
▸ A note on partner mental health (yes, you too)
▸ A clip-and-keep one-page summary card

WHY I WROTE THIS

Because every postpartum guide tells partners to "be there." That's
not actionable. This is.

Because the loneliest hour of the fourth trimester is 3am when she's
nursing and you're trying to figure out if you should be awake too
(yes — read why on page 14).

Because the version of you 6 months from now will be glad you
ordered the right thing in week one instead of looking it up at 4am
while she's crying.

WHO THIS IS FOR

▸ Expecting dads
▸ Non-birthing partners of any kind
▸ Anyone whose partner is pregnant
▸ Yourself, if your friend's wife is having a baby and you don't
  know what to send

WHO IT ISN'T FOR

▸ The birthing parent. Give this to your partner. Make them read
  it. The point is for THEM to internalize it, not for you to.

WHAT YOU GET

▸ Instant download PDF, 24 pages, US Letter + A4
▸ Low-ink home printing optimized
▸ Personal use only
▸ Yours forever in your Gumroad library

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GENTLE DISCLAIMER

This is a planning guide based on general experience and synthesis —
not medical, psychological, or legal advice. For your specific
situation, talk to a healthcare provider. If your partner sounds
like she's drowning, the US Maternal Mental Health Hotline is
1-833-852-6262.

— the shop

p.s. If you're getting this for someone you love — bonus points for
also getting them the "Saying No" Script Pack (it's the partner /
birthing-parent combo). Or read it yourself and absorb it; that's
the better gift.
```

**Bundle upsell:** Bundle with P02 (Saying No Scripts) = $14 instead of $16. Captures both members of the couple.

**After-purchase email:** Short. Subject: *"the only page you need to read first"*. Body points them to "Week 1 — Survival mode" + the conversation-to-have-before-baby checklist on page 22.
