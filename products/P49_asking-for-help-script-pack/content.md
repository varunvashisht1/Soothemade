# The Asking for Help Script Pack

*Twenty pages, thirty scripts. The companion to Saying No.*

---

## A note before you start

Asking for help is harder than saying no.

Saying no is one word. Asking for help is a request that lands on another person. It risks rejection. It risks the wrong help showing up. It risks looking like you cannot manage on your own. So most people who need help do not ask. They white-knuckle through, or they wait for someone to offer, which mostly doesn't happen.

This pack is the words. Thirty scripts. Specific people. Specific requests. Specific times. Easy ways for the other person to say no without it being awkward.

Because the difference between a successful ask and a failed one is rarely the relationship. It is the specificity.

*With care, Maya*

> *This is a planning tool. Asking for help around financial matters does not replace a financial planner. Asking for emotional help does not replace a therapist. The scripts route to professionals where relevant.*

## How to use this pack

- Find the script that fits the kind of help you need.
- Substitute the specifics for your situation.
- Send it (text, email, or phone, depending on who).
- The person can say yes or no. Both are okay. The script is designed to make "no" easy too.

A small principle: ask early. Ask before you collapse. The version of you that asks at week 4 is asking from a stronger position than the version of you that finally asks at week 8 after a meltdown.

## The structure of a good ask

Every good ask has four parts.

```
1. SPECIFIC PERSON

   Not "anyone." Not "the group chat." A specific person, by name.

2. SPECIFIC REQUEST

   Not "help." A specific thing.
   "Could you bring dinner Thursday."
   "Could you watch the baby for one hour Tuesday afternoon."
   "Could you come sit with me Saturday morning."

3. SPECIFIC TIME

   A date or a time window. Not "sometime soon."
   "This Thursday around 6pm."
   "Tuesday between 2 and 3pm."
   "Saturday at 10am."

4. EASY EXIT

   A clause that makes "no" comfortable.
   "Totally fine if you cannot."
   "No worries if you have other plans."
   "If this doesn't work, no pressure at all."

EVERY SCRIPT IN THIS PACK FOLLOWS THIS STRUCTURE. YOURS CAN TOO.
```

## Meals

The most common, most useful kind of help.

```
SCRIPT 1, MEAL DROP-OFF (to a friend)

   "Hi [name], I am still getting our footing here in the early weeks
   with the baby. Would you be able to drop off a meal sometime this
   week? Any night between [date] and [date] would be great. Even a
   takeout pickup at your favorite spot would be amazing. No
   pressure if this doesn't work."

SCRIPT 2, MEAL DROP-OFF (to a family member)

   "Mom / [name], I need a hand with dinners this week. Could you
   bring something Thursday around 6? If you're up for it, dinner
   would help us so much. No need to stay long, we just need food
   that we did not have to cook."

SCRIPT 3, MEAL TRAIN COORDINATION (to a close friend who can organize)

   "Hi [name], would you be willing to set up a small meal-train link
   for us? We don't want a huge production. Maybe a meal every other
   day for two weeks. I'll send you the list of who to invite if you
   are open to it. If this is too much to coordinate, no pressure,
   I'll figure something else out."

SCRIPT 4, GROCERY RUN

   "[Name], I'm at the point where the grocery run is the thing I
   can't quite manage. Would you be willing to pick up a list for
   me this week? I'll Venmo you for everything. Could mean a lot
   of help, no pressure if it doesn't work."

SCRIPT 5, PROFESSIONAL DELIVERY

   "[Name], I know this is random, but is there a chance I could
   ask you to send us a few weeks of grocery delivery or meal-kit
   subscription as our baby gift? It would be the best gift in the
   world right now. Of course if not, no pressure at all."
```

## Childcare

For someone watching the kids, even briefly.

```
SCRIPT 6, ONE HOUR FOR A PARENT'S OWN APPOINTMENT

   "[Name], I have a doctor's appointment Tuesday at 2pm. Could you
   come over and be with [baby] for one hour, 1:45 to 2:45? They will
   probably nap most of that time. I'll have everything set up.
   Totally fine if you can't."

SCRIPT 7, MORNING FOR A REST

   "Mom / [name], could you come over Saturday morning, say 9 to 11,
   and just hold [baby] / be with [older kid] while I sleep? No need
   to do anything. Just be there with them. This would help more
   than you know."

SCRIPT 8, KID PICK-UP FROM SCHOOL/DAYCARE

   "[Name], something came up and I need to be at [appointment]
   Thursday afternoon. Would you be able to pick [kid] up from
   [school/daycare] at 3pm and keep them for an hour or two until I
   can get there? I'll write [school] to add you to the pickup list.
   No worries if not."

SCRIPT 9, AN HOUR FOR A SHOWER

   "[Name], this is small but real, I haven't showered in three days.
   Could you come over for one hour [day] so I can shower, eat
   something, and breathe? I am not asking you to do anything except
   be in the house with me."

SCRIPT 10, OVERNIGHT (for the brave ask)

   "[Name], I am wondering if you would be open to keeping [older
   kid] overnight on [date]. [Partner] and I really need one stretch
   of sleep. They have school the next morning so they would just
   need their backpack and breakfast. Completely understand if this
   is too much to ask."
```

## Eldercare

For the adult child caring for an aging parent.

```
SCRIPT 11, COMPANY FOR THE PARENT FOR AN AFTERNOON

   "[Sibling name], could you spend the afternoon with Mom / Dad on
   [date]? I have not had a day off in weeks. I'd be so grateful if
   you could be there from say 11 to 4. They would love to see you.
   Let me know if that works."

SCRIPT 12, TAKING OVER FOR A WEEKEND

   "[Sibling], I am asking for a real weekend off. Could you take
   over for the weekend of [date]? You don't have to be at the
   house the whole time, but you would be the primary point of
   contact for any issues with Mom / Dad, including any calls from
   the facility / home aide. Two days. I need a real break."

SCRIPT 13, ACCOMPANYING TO AN APPOINTMENT

   "[Name], Mom has an appointment with [specialist] on [date] at
   [time]. Could you come with us? I need a second pair of ears in
   the room, and someone who can drive home if I am too rattled by
   what they tell us."
```

## Errands

The small daily logistics.

```
SCRIPT 14, PHARMACY OR PICK-UP

   "[Name], could you grab a [prescription / package / dry cleaning]
   for me this week? It's just at [location]. I'll Venmo you for any
   pickup fee. Totally fine if you can't."

SCRIPT 15, RIDE TO AN APPOINTMENT

   "[Name], I have an appointment [date] at [time] across town. Could
   you drive me? I don't think I should drive home after. I'll buy
   you coffee on the way. No worries if you're busy."

SCRIPT 16, AN AIRPORT RUN

   "[Name], I have to get to the airport at [time] on [date]. Any
   chance you can drive me / pick me up? I know it's a lift, no
   pressure at all if not."
```

## Emotional support

When you need a person, not a task.

```
SCRIPT 17, JUST TALKING ON THE PHONE

   "[Name], I am having a hard week. Could you call me sometime in
   the next day or two and just let me talk? You don't have to fix
   anything. I just need to hear your voice."

SCRIPT 18, A VISIT WHERE YOU DO NOTHING TOGETHER

   "[Name], could you come over [day] and just be here? We don't
   need to do anything. We can sit on the couch. We can fold laundry.
   I am tired of being alone with my own thoughts."

SCRIPT 19, A WALK

   "[Name], could we walk [date] at [time]? An hour, somewhere we
   both like. I just need to be outside with another adult for a bit."

SCRIPT 20, A CRY OVER TEA

   "[Name], I am not okay. Can I come over [time] and cry over tea?
   You don't have to do anything. I just need to be near you for a
   bit."

A NOTE: IF YOU ARE STRUGGLING IN A WAY THAT IS BIGGER THAN A FRIEND
CAN HOLD, A THERAPIST IS A DIFFERENT TYPE OF ASK. ASKING FOR HELP
INCLUDES ASKING FOR PROFESSIONAL HELP WHEN THE WEIGHT IS LARGER
THAN ANY FRIEND OUGHT TO HANDLE. CRISIS LINES IN THE US: 988. UK:
116 123. CANADA: 9-8-8.
```

## Money

The hardest ask category. Even the scripts here come with a note that financial professionals exist.

```
SCRIPT 21, ASKING A FAMILY MEMBER FOR A LOAN

   "[Name], I am in a tight spot and would like to ask you for a
   short-term loan. I am looking for $[amount], to be repaid by
   [date] on [terms]. I would like to put this in writing so it's
   clear for both of us. I will completely understand if this is not
   possible. I want to ask in a way that respects our relationship
   either way."

SCRIPT 22, ASKING A FRIEND TO COVER A SHARED EXPENSE TEMPORARILY

   "[Name], I am cash-flow tight right now. Could you cover my half
   of [shared expense] this month? I'll pay you back by [date].
   Totally fine if not, I'll figure it out otherwise."

SCRIPT 23, ASKING FOR HELP FROM A PROFESSIONAL OR EMPLOYER

   "[Name / HR], I am dealing with a temporary financial strain and
   wanted to ask if [the company] has any short-term assistance,
   advance, or hardship programs available. Even pointing me to the
   right resource would help."

A NOTE: BEFORE ASKING FAMILY OR FRIENDS FOR MONEY, CONSIDER WHETHER
A FINANCIAL PLANNER, A CREDIT-COUNSELING SERVICE, OR A SOCIAL WORKER
COULD HELP YOU SEE OPTIONS YOU MIGHT NOT KNOW EXIST. MONEY ASKS
BETWEEN INDIVIDUALS CAN STRAIN RELATIONSHIPS. THE PROFESSIONAL ROUTE
IS SOMETIMES KINDER.
```

## Home help

Cleaning, repairs, lawn, the things that build up.

```
SCRIPT 24, A HOUSE-CLEANING HAND

   "[Name], could you help me deep-clean the house [day]? Even just
   two hours together. I'll make us lunch after. I need company, not
   a magic act."

SCRIPT 25, A SMALL REPAIR

   "[Name], I know you're handy. Would you be willing to come help
   me with [specific repair: a leaky faucet, a curtain rod, hanging
   shelves] sometime in the next two weeks? I'll cover the parts and
   feed you afterward."

SCRIPT 26, LAUNDRY HELP

   "[Name], any chance you could come over Saturday and help me get
   through the laundry pile? It's gotten out of hand. We can do it
   together. I'll provide snacks."

SCRIPT 27, ORGANIZING A ROOM

   "[Name], I want to attack [the kid's closet / the garage / the
   pantry] this month and I would not get it done alone. Could you
   spend an afternoon with me on it? Sunday or Saturday."
```

## Visit

The "just come over" ask.

```
SCRIPT 28, COMING OVER WITHOUT EXPECTATIONS

   "[Name], can you come over [day] and hang out for a few hours?
   I do not want to leave the house. We can watch a movie / sit on
   the porch / drink coffee. Bring nothing. Just come."

SCRIPT 29, A VIDEO CALL

   "[Name], could we do a video call this week? I miss seeing your
   face. Even half an hour would help. Tell me what works for you."

SCRIPT 30, "WILL YOU SIT WITH ME"

   "[Name], I am not feeling great. Will you come over and sit with
   me for an hour? You don't have to talk."

A REMINDER: THE THIRTY SCRIPTS ARE TEMPLATES. EDIT FREELY. THE
NUMBER OF PEOPLE WHO WILL SAY YES IS HIGHER THAN YOU THINK. THE
NUMBER WHO WILL SAY NO IS ALSO REAL AND DOES NOT MEAN ANYTHING ABOUT
YOU OR ABOUT THEM.
```

## When they say no

Sometimes the answer will be no. That has to be okay, or asking does not work.

```
WHAT TO SAY BACK WHEN SOMEONE DECLINES

   "Totally understood, thank you for letting me know."

   "No worries at all, I appreciate you considering it."

   "I get it, thanks for being honest."

WHAT NOT TO SAY

   - "Are you sure?" (Pressures them.)
   - "It's fine, I'll just do it myself." (Guilt-trippy.)
   - "I just thought you would want to help." (Manipulative.)

WHEN MULTIPLE PEOPLE SAY NO

   That is information, not a verdict.

   Sometimes it means:
   - Your network is stretched thin (their lives are also hard)
   - You need a different network for this kind of help
   - You need a paid version of this help (a postpartum doula, a
     respite worker, a cleaner, a meal-delivery service)

WHEN NO ONE TO ASK COMES TO MIND

   That is its own data. Worth talking to a therapist or social
   worker about. Isolation is a real problem, and there are services
   designed for people without local networks.

   A REMINDER FOR EITHER CASE: PAID HELP IS HELP. IT IS NOT A FAILURE
   OF YOUR NETWORK. IT IS A DIFFERENT TOOL.
```

## When they say yes

The "yes" needs to be received well so they can do it again.

```
WHAT TO SAY BACK WHEN SOMEONE SAYS YES

   "Oh, thank you. This is going to help so much."

   "I really appreciate it. [Specific thing: I'll see you Thursday at
   6, no need to bring anything else]."

   "This is the kindest thing. Truly."

WHAT TO MAKE EASY FOR THEM

   - Tell them exactly where to be and when.
   - If they're bringing food, tell them what counts as a successful
     meal in your house. They will guess wrong otherwise.
   - If they're watching the kid, leave a one-page note with what
     the kid eats, naps, calms with, gets scared of.
   - If they're running an errand, send the list with quantities
     and brands you trust.

   The friend who feels successful doing one ask is the friend who
   says yes to the next ask.

WHAT TO NOT DO

   - Do not change your mind at the last minute unless something
     genuinely came up. The friend who arrived to find you "all set"
     after preparing for two hours will be less available next time.
   - Do not over-apologize while they are there. Apologizing 20 times
     for "putting them out" makes them feel awkward. One thank-you
     at the start, one at the end, in between just be present.

THANK-YOU AFTER

   - A text the next day with one specific thank-you.
   - A small handwritten note for the bigger asks.
   - A small reciprocal favor when you are back on your feet.

THE GOAL IS NOT SCOREKEEPING. THE GOAL IS A RELATIONSHIP THAT CAN
HOLD MUTUAL HELP OVER A LIFETIME.
```

## Thank-you script library

For when the help has happened.

```
TEXT, NEXT-DAY THANK-YOU

   "Hey, just wanted to say thank you again for [specific thing].
   It really helped. I owe you one."

TEXT, A WEEK LATER

   "Still thinking about how much your [help] meant. Thank you."

WRITTEN NOTE, FOR BIGGER HELP

   "Dear [Name],

   I want you to know what you did meant more than I could say in
   the moment. [One specific detail of the help they gave.] I will
   not forget it.

   With love and thanks,
   [Your name]"

VOICE NOTE OR CALL, FOR THE BIGGEST HELP

   Sometimes the right thank-you is your voice. Not a text. Tell
   them.

A SMALL REPAY GIFT (when ready)

   - A meal you make for them later.
   - A book you know they will love.
   - Childcare for them when their season turns hard.
   - Something specific to the kind of help they gave.

THE MOST IMPORTANT REPAY IS THIS: THE NEXT TIME THIS PERSON SAYS
YES TO HELP YOU, SAY YES BACK WHEN THEY NEED YOU. THE RECIPROCITY
IS WHAT TURNS A FAVOR INTO A RELATIONSHIP.
```

## A final note

You asked.

That is the hardest part. Not the receiving of help. Not the thanking afterward. The asking.

You did the thing that people who do not ask never do. You named a need to another person. You gave them the dignity of being asked. That is its own gift to them, even if they said no.

The next time will be slightly easier. The time after that, slightly more.

The thirty scripts are templates. The way you use them is yours. The relationships you build with these asks will outlast this season.

You are not alone, even when the work feels like it. The asking is the bridge.

*With care, Maya*
