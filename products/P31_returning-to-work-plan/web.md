Returning to work after a baby is a transition that the workplace, the partner, the school, and your own body all have feelings about. The Back-to-Work Pumping Planner (P17) handles the feeding logistics. This is the broader question: the schedule, the identity, the meetings, the new dynamic with whoever you used to be at work.

This planner is for the first day back and the next ninety. It assumes the body is doing what it is doing. It assumes you are not the same worker you were a year ago. It assumes the workplace will not adjust to that fact on its own.

## What's inside

Thirty-six pages, undated:

- **The two-weeks-before checklist.** The manager conversation. The HR check-in. The childcare confirmation. The "in case of" plan.
- **The first-day-back script.** What you say when people ask how the baby is. What you say when someone leans in too close. What you say when the work is way behind where you left it.
- **The new morning routine.** Reverse-engineered from "what time do I need to leave" backward, in 15-minute blocks.
- **The new evening routine.** Same approach.
- **The "I am also a different worker now" page.** The capacity has shifted. The values have shifted. The patience has shifted. None of those are weaknesses.
- **A meeting-load worksheet.** What you can actually take on this quarter. What you cannot. Re-do every two months.
- **The first-week check-in.** Five lines a day. What was hard. What surprised you. What you need to ask for.
- **The thirty-day check-in.** Wider lens. The work, the body, the home.
- **The ninety-day check-in.** Decision page: this is sustainable, this needs to change, this is the limit.
- **A "this isn't working" page.** Honest options. Conversations with your manager. Reducing hours. Switching roles. Leaving. None of those are failure.
- **A page for the partner.** What you need them to handle without negotiation in the first month back.

## Who this is for

You, with one week of leave left, dreading the return.

You, three weeks back, realizing this is harder than you knew.

You, six months back, considering whether to stay.

You, in a job that does not fit the parent you have become. You may want a new one. You may want to make this one fit. Both are real.
