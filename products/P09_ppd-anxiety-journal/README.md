# P09 — Postpartum Anxiety + PPD Self-Awareness Journal

> Wave 2 product. High demand. **Most YMYL-sensitive product in the catalog.** Owner MUST re-read `content.md` fully before publishing.

## Product summary

A 60-page journal designed to help new parents notice, name, and track postpartum mental-health changes — and to make it easier to talk to a provider about what they're experiencing. **Not a diagnostic tool. Not a substitute for therapy.** A tracking + self-awareness companion.

Includes a daily mood log, weekly self-check inventory (our own simplified language — does NOT reproduce the EPDS or any copyrighted instrument), CBT-style noticing prompts, a "when to call your provider" trigger list, an appointment-prep page, and a partner observation page.

- **Pages:** 60
- **Price:** $14 (priced for emotional WTP; some buyers will gift it)
- **Buyer:** new parents 0–12 months postpartum + their partners + therapists looking for a client take-home tool
- **Format:** PDF, undated

## YMYL — read this carefully

This product touches mental-health territory. Voice in `content.md` is built around these rules:

1. **Never diagnose.** No "you have PPD if you score X." We use the word "notice" everywhere, never "diagnose."
2. **Never replace professional help.** Every section ends pointing the reader toward a provider.
3. **Crisis-aware.** 988 + Maternal Mental Health Hotline 1-833-852-6262 appear at the front, on every "concerning signs" page, and in the footer.
4. **Don't reproduce copyrighted instruments.** The "weekly check-in" is our own plain-language inventory. It is NOT the EPDS, NOT the PHQ-9. We point readers to those tools when appropriate — administered by a clinician.
5. **Inclusive of all parents.** PPD/PPA affect non-birthing partners too. Language doesn't assume birthing parent only.

The owner should consider having one of the following review `content.md` before publishing:
- A perinatal mental-health-certified clinician (PMH-C)
- A licensed therapist who works with new parents
- At minimum: a friend who has been treated for PPD/PPA, asking specifically "does anything in here feel triggering, prescriptive, or misleading?"
