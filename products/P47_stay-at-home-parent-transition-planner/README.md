# P47 — Stay-at-Home Parent Transition Planner

A 30-page planner for someone leaving paid work to stay home with kids, or moving to a reduced work schedule. Covers the identity work, the financial conversation, the day-shape design, the friend-network rebuilding, and the eventual re-entry planning.

## Why this exists

The transition from paid work to stay-at-home parent is one of the largest unspoken transitions in modern parenthood. Most planning resources focus on the kid. This one focuses on the parent — what they will do with the day, how the money conversation needs to land, how their identity will need to re-knit, and how to plan for re-entry if and when that comes.

## YMYL care

- Non-YMYL.
- Mental-health page references depression/isolation as elevated risk for SAH parents and routes to therapy + crisis lines.
- Financial pages route to a financial planner for any specific moves.

## Scope

Approximately 30 pages:

- Cover, 3 front-matter pages
- The "I am still a person" identity page
- Pre-transition: the partner conversation about money
- The retirement + own-money page (often the most-skipped)
- The "what if we split" honest planning page
- The "what is my work now" reframe
- Day-shape design (templates for different ages of kids)
- Friend-network rebuilding
- Social/professional identity outside of parenting
- Re-entry planning (when and how to go back, if you want to)
- The "I am not okay" page
- Crisis lines
- A final letter
