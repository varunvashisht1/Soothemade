# P37 — Pediatrician Visit Prep Cards

Twelve cards, one per well-baby / well-child visit from newborn through age two. Each card describes what the pediatrician usually checks, what to ask, what to write down after, and the red flags to watch for between visits.

## Why this exists

The well-baby appointment schedule is dense in the first two years (about twelve visits in the US AAP schedule). Each visit has its own checklist of measurements, milestones, conversations, and vaccines. Parents arrive tired, forget the questions they wanted to ask, and leave with notes scribbled on the back of a parking-receipt. These cards are the structured prep + record so the parent walks in with their questions and walks out with the answers written down.

## YMYL care

- Cards do NOT name vaccines by brand or generic. Vaccines are referred to as "the routine vaccines for this visit" and the parent is routed to the pediatrician for the current schedule.
- No medication names elsewhere. Fevers, rashes, breathing changes all route to the pediatrician.
- Red flags listed are widely-known pediatric warning signs ("any fever under 3 months", "trouble breathing", "lethargy", "not feeding"), framed conservatively.

## Scope

Approximately 20 pages including:

- Cover, parent note, how-to-use the cards, "what to expect at every visit" page
- 12 visit cards (one per page):
  - Newborn (in hospital or 24-48 hr after discharge)
  - 3 to 5 day visit
  - 1 to 2 week visit
  - 1 month
  - 2 months
  - 4 months
  - 6 months
  - 9 months
  - 12 months (1 year)
  - 15 months
  - 18 months
  - 24 months (2 year)
- "When to call between visits" page
- A final note
