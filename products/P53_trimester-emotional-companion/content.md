# A Trimester-by-Trimester Emotional Companion

*Thirty slow pages. For the feelings no medical chart tracks.*

---

## A note before you start

Pregnancy planners mostly track the body. Weight. Symptoms. Appointments. Kicks. Weeks.

This one tracks the feelings.

You are doing something enormous. You are becoming a person you have not been before. You are grieving the version of you that fits in the small chair at the cafe, the one who could drink the second glass of wine, the one who slept through the night, the one who did not have to think about a body that is now two bodies.

You are also, possibly, excited. Curious. Tired in a new way. Bonded to a child you have not met. Scared. Hopeful. Sometimes all of that in one Tuesday afternoon.

The medical chart has no row for any of this. This journal does.

*Soothemade*

> *This is a journaling companion, not therapy. If the feelings get heavier than journaling can hold (intrusive thoughts, persistent sadness, inability to function), please see a perinatal mental-health therapist. The "if you are not okay" page at the back has crisis lines.*

---

## How to use this companion

- Open it slowly. Each page has time built in.
- Five prompted pages per trimester. Do them in order, or skip around.
- Use a real pen, if you can. Typing is not the same.
- Some prompts are not answerable in one sitting. Come back.
- This is not a public-facing artifact. Write the honest thing.

---

## This is the slow journal

A page to set the tone.

```
THE TWO JOURNALS

There is the journal where you write down what the OB said today.
There is this journal, where you write down what you have not said
to anyone.

THE OPPOSITE OF EFFICIENCY

The fast journal is for tracking. This is for feeling. The fast
journal helps you remember what week you are in. This helps you
remember what it FELT like to be in that week.

A FEW GROUND RULES

- You can write something embarrassing.
- You can write something you would never say out loud.
- You can write a thing and then change your mind a week later.
- You can write the angry thing about your mother or your partner.
- You can write that you are not sure you want this.
- You can write that you are sure you do.
- Both can be true in different weeks.

A SMALL CEREMONY

   _______________________________________________

   _______________________________________________

A NOTE: TWO TRUE THINGS AT ONCE

   "I am happy to be having this baby."
   "I am grieving the version of my life that is closing."

   Both can be true. Most pregnant people who feel them both at
   once think they are wrong to. They are not. The grief is the
   cost of how full your life was before. It honors what you had.

## If you are not okay

The companion is a journaling tool. It is not a substitute for mental-health care.

```
HONEST CHECK

   Am I able to enjoy things that used to bring me joy?
       ☐ Yes  ☐ Sometimes  ☐ Not really

   Am I sleeping?
       ☐ Yes  ☐ Some  ☐ No

   Am I eating?
       ☐ Yes  ☐ Some  ☐ No

   Am I having thoughts that are scaring me?
       ☐ No  ☐ Yes

   Am I avoiding prenatal care because of anxiety?
       ☐ No  ☐ Yes

   Has anyone close to me expressed concern about me?
       ☐ No  ☐ Yes

If two or more of these point toward "not okay," see a perinatal
mental-health professional. Perinatal depression and anxiety affect
roughly 1 in 5 pregnant people. The treatment is good. The treatment
is short. Pregnancy is not the time to wait it out.

## A final letter

You wrote down what no chart tracks.

The feelings, the small moments, the version of yourself you noticed shifting, the friendships that bent under the weight of the change, the body becoming visible to strangers, the night the baby kicked and you knew, the morning you missed your old life.

In a year, you will read this and remember things you would have lost otherwise. The first cup of coffee you skipped. The friend who showed up. The hard week. The Saturday morning you wrote three pages and then napped.

This is not the journal that helps you remember what week you were in. It is the one that helps you remember what it FELT like to be in that week.

Both kinds of remembering matter. This one matters more, often, than the chart.

*Soothemade*
