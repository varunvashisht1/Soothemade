# P41 — Discreet TTC Fertility Journal

A 60-page fertility-tracking journal that passes as a generic daily-notes book if anyone flips through it. For people TTC who do not want their MIL, roommate, parents, or coworkers to glance at the cover and know.

## Why this exists

Fertility tracking surfaces are usually obvious. The cover says "fertility journal" or "TTC tracker." The interior says "cycle day 4, ovulation predicted by..." The buyer cannot leave it on the kitchen counter without it being a topic of conversation.

This journal solves that. The cover is a neutral "Daily Notes Journal." The interior uses neutral language ("body temperature," "today's body note," "energy") to track the same data a clinical tracker would (BBT, cervical fluid, symptoms). The buyer who knows what they bought can do real fertility tracking. The casual flip-through reader sees a wellness journal.

A short "what each field is" decode page is included for the buyer. It is at the back and can be torn out if they want the journal to be fully ambiguous.

## YMYL care

- Low-YMYL. The journal is a tracking surface, not a diagnostic tool.
- It does not name medications.
- It does not predict ovulation or interpret the data. The buyer's healthcare provider does that.
- A "when to talk to a reproductive endocrinologist" page at the back routes the buyer to clinical care if 6 to 12 months of tracking are not surfacing what they hoped.

## Scope

Approximately 60 pages including:

- Cover (discreet title)
- Front matter (3 pages: a note, how to use, what each field is)
- Six cycles of tracking, each:
  - Cycle overview page (numbered 1, 2, 3 etc.)
  - Four weekly spread pages (7 days each row)
  - One reflection page
  - One next-cycle-planning page
- Reference pages: pattern notes, symptom dictionary, when to talk to a provider
- Tear-out decode key page (clearly marked)
- A final note
