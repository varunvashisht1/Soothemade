## Listing description

```
The thing nobody told me about the fourth trimester is that I'd run
out of words.

Not the big words. The small ones. The ones you use to tell your
mother-in-law you're not ready for her to come over. The ones you
use when someone says "you look tired" and you want to scream. The
ones you need at the door when your aunt drops by unannounced and
your stitches still hurt.

I made this for the version of me — and probably you — who knows
exactly what she wants to say but can't find the sentence.

WHAT'S INSIDE
- 30 ready-to-use scripts across 6 categories:
  - Hospital visitors (5)
  - First week at home (5)
  - Unsolicited advice (8)
  - Visitor-pressure family (6)
  - Photos & social media (3)
  - Body & emotional commentary (3)

- 5 longer templates for the hard conversations:
  - The pre-baby family heads-up text
  - The "I changed my mind" follow-up
  - The "we need space" honest conversation
  - The "no gifts at the shower" ask
  - The "thanks but no" gift script

- A short guide on how to use the scripts without justifying yourself

- A short note at the end on what to do when people are hurt by your boundaries

HOW THIS HELPS
Postpartum exhaustion eats words first. You know what you want.
You just can't find the sentence. This pack gives you the sentences.

You don't have to use them word-for-word. Modify them. Hand them
to your partner. Print and pin to the door.

WHO THIS IS FOR
- Late-pregnancy parents preparing for the fourth trimester
- New parents who keep saying yes to visits they don't want
- Anyone whose family has "strong opinions"
- Friends and family — yes, this makes a thoughtful baby shower gift
  (the most useful gift, frankly)

WHAT YOU GET (instant digital download)
- 1 PDF, 20 pages, US Letter
- 1 PDF, 20 pages, A4
- Black on warm-ivory background, optimized for low-ink home printing
- Print or use on screen

NOTES
- This is a finished PDF, not an editable template.
- Personal use only.
- No physical product ships — instant digital download after purchase.

GENTLE DISCLAIMER
This is a planning tool, not a substitute for therapy, legal advice,
or medical advice. If you're struggling, please reach out to a
professional. The US Maternal Mental Health Hotline is
1-833-852-6262.

— Soothemade
```
