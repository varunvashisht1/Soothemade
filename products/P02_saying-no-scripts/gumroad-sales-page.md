## Sales-page body (long-form)

```
The 4th time the doorbell rang that day, I started crying.

It wasn't the doorbell. It was that I'd just gotten the baby to sleep
for the first time in six hours. It was that the laundry pile on the
chair was a real-life mountain. It was that I was bleeding through
another giant maxi pad and my stitches still itched. It was that I
hadn't eaten anything but cold toast that whole day.

And someone — well-meaning, beloved, and entirely unaware — was
standing on my porch holding a casserole dish wanting to "just pop
in for ten minutes."

I knew exactly what I wanted to say.

I couldn't find a single word.

I made this pack when I realized I'd been saying yes to visits I
didn't want, advice I hadn't asked for, and "we're already in the
car" texts I hadn't recovered from — not because I didn't know how
to set a boundary, but because postpartum exhaustion had eaten my
sentences.

If that sounds familiar, this is for you.

WHAT IT IS

A 20-page printable PDF with 30 ready-to-use scripts and 5 longer
templates for the conversations you keep putting off. Use them
exactly as written. Modify them. Hand them to your partner. Print
and tape one to the front door.

You don't have to justify yourself. You don't have to explain. You
just have to say the words. And these are the words.

WHAT'S INCLUDED

▸ 30 scripts across 6 categories:
   ▹ Hospital visitors (5)
   ▹ First week at home (5)
   ▹ Unsolicited advice (8)
   ▹ Visitor-pressure family (6)
   ▹ Photos & social media (3)
   ▹ Body & emotional commentary (3)

▸ 5 longer templates:
   ▹ The pre-baby family heads-up text
   ▹ The "I changed my mind" follow-up
   ▹ The "we need space" hard conversation
   ▹ The "no gifts at the shower" ask
   ▹ The "thanks but no" gift script

▸ A short guide on how to use the scripts (and how NOT to use them)

▸ A note at the end on what to do when people are hurt by your
   boundaries — because some of them will be

FORMAT

▸ Instant digital download (PDF)
▸ 20 pages, US Letter + A4 versions both included
▸ Print or use on screen
▸ Designed for low-ink home printing
▸ Personal use only

WHO THIS IS FOR

You, if you're a few weeks from your due date and your in-laws are
already asking about the hospital visit.

You, if you're 3 weeks postpartum and the visitor text chain is
starting to make your stomach hurt.

You, if your mother means well but cannot stop herself from telling
you you're holding the baby wrong.

You, if you're looking for a baby shower gift that's actually useful
— and not another swaddle. (This is the most useful gift, frankly.
We'll wait.)

You, if you keep saying yes when you mean no, and you can't figure
out why.

WHY I MADE THIS

Because every postpartum "boundaries" article tells you to set
boundaries — and then never gives you the actual words. Because the
people who need this most are the most depleted. Because exhaustion
isn't a personal failure, and lack of words isn't a character flaw.

You shouldn't have to invent a sentence at 3am when someone texts
"can I come hold the baby today?" You should be able to pick the
sentence.

▸ Not a therapy substitute. If you're struggling, please reach

▸ Not legal advice. Some scripts touch on photos/social media —

▸ Not a one-size-fits-all rulebook. You're going to modify these.

This is for personal use. Please don't resell or redistribute.
```
