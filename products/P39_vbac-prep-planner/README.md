# P39 — VBAC Prep Planner

A 24-page planner for someone considering a VBAC (vaginal birth after cesarean) or being asked to consider a repeat cesarean. Covers the emotional weight of the previous birth, the provider conversation prep, the hospital-policy questions, the decision log, and the "if labor goes a different way" page.

## Why this exists

VBAC decisions get politicized fast. The internet, the in-laws, the doula, the OB, and the friend who just had one all have opinions, often loud ones, often contradictory. The actual decision belongs to the person and their provider, and most existing planners do not respect the complexity of that decision space. They either advocate for VBAC or against it.

This planner stays neutral. The decision is the person's. The planner is the surface where the questions get asked and the answers get written down.

## YMYL care

- Cover carries the YMYL banner.
- The planner does **not** recommend VBAC or recommend against it.
- No statistics. (Statistics are situation-specific. The provider has them for this person.)
- No medication names.
- Explicit "this planner does not make this decision for you" page.
- Trauma-aware pages for the previous cesarean experience.
- Emergency-cesarean planning page included so the planner is not assuming TOLAC succeeds.

## Scope

Approximately 24 pages:

- Cover, 3 front-matter pages including the "we are not recommending" page
- The previous cesarean: revisiting page (trauma-aware)
- Finding / confirming a VBAC-supportive provider page
- Hospital + practice policy questions page
- Questions to bring to your provider (multiple categories)
- The TOLAC eligibility conversation page
- Decision-tracking log (your specific decision over time)
- Late-pregnancy timeline page
- The "if labor starts" page
- The "if it converts to cesarean" page
- The "if I decide to schedule a repeat cesarean" page
- Partner page
- A final letter
