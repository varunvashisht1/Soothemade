# A VBAC Prep Planner

*Twenty-four pages for the decision that is not actually yours alone. Tools for the conversation. Space for the feelings. Plans for both paths.*

---

## A note before you start

You are facing a decision that comes with a lot of noise.

Maybe you had a cesarean for your first birth, and this time you want a vaginal delivery if it is safe for you to attempt. Maybe you are not sure. Maybe your provider is recommending one direction, and you are not sure if you agree. Maybe you are getting opinions from people who have no business having opinions on your body.

This planner does not have an opinion either. It is not pro-VBAC. It is not pro-repeat-cesarean. The decision is between you and your provider, in conversation, over the course of this pregnancy. Some decisions get made early. Some get revisited late. Some get made during labor. All of those are legitimate.

What the planner does is hold the conversation. The questions you want to bring. The answers you get back. The decision as it is right now (which may not be the decision a month from now). The plans for whichever way it goes.

*Soothemade*

> *This is a planning and tracking tool, not medical advice. The decision to attempt a vaginal birth after cesarean or to schedule a repeat cesarean is medical, situation-specific, and belongs to you and your obstetric provider. This planner does not recommend either direction. It records the conversation.*

---

## How to use this planner

- Read the front matter first, including the "we are not recommending" page.
- The trauma page is optional. Skip it if your first cesarean was uncomplicated and you do not need to revisit it.
- Bring the question pages to your appointments. Write the answers down in the planner, not on a sticky note.
- Update the decision log every few weeks. The decision can change. That is normal.
- Both back-half pages, "if labor starts" and "if it converts to cesarean," are equal weight. The planner does not assume one outcome.

---

## This planner is not making the decision

It is worth being explicit.

This planner does not tell you to attempt a VBAC. It does not tell you to schedule a repeat cesarean. It does not list percentages of success. It does not have a quiz that calculates your "VBAC score." It does not provide medical advice.

Why not.

Because the decision is situation-specific. The relevant factors include the reason for the previous cesarean, the type of uterine incision, the time since the previous birth, your current health, the baby's position, the hospital's policies, the provider's experience, and your own preferences. None of those things can be evaluated by a planner. They are evaluated by your provider, with you, in conversation.

What the planner can do:

- Help you prepare for that conversation
- Hold the answers you get
- Track your evolving stance
- Plan for both outcomes

What the planner refuses to do:

- Tell you what to choose
- Promise a specific outcome
- Quote statistics that may not apply to you

You do not have to be "over" the first birth to make a decision about this one. You are allowed to bring all of it.

## Finding or confirming your provider

Not every provider supports VBAC. Some practices flatly do not. Some support it conditionally. Some have a lot of experience. Some have none.


## Hospital + practice policy questions

Hospitals vary widely. Some have a labor-and-delivery setup that supports VBAC. Some require a 24-hour in-house OB. Some require specific staff coverage. Some flatly do not allow VBAC after a certain hour. Knowing the policy ahead of time prevents surprise.

```
HOSPITAL QUESTIONS

☐ Does this hospital allow TOLAC?
☐ Are there time-of-day restrictions on when TOLAC can be done?
   (Some hospitals only allow it when an OB is in-house.)
☐ What monitoring is required during a TOLAC?
☐ How quickly can an emergency cesarean be performed if needed?
☐ Is anesthesia in-house 24/7?
☐ How many TOLACs do you do per month, roughly?
☐ What is the success rate at this hospital? (Take any number with
   context, but a hospital that does many TOLACs often has more
   experience.)

THIS HOSPITAL

   Name:                       __________________________
   Their TOLAC policy summary, in their words:
       _______________________________________________
       _______________________________________________

   Any restrictions I should know about:
       _______________________________________________
```

## The "if it converts to cesarean" page

A TOLAC that becomes a cesarean is not a failed TOLAC. It is a labor that ended a different way.

This page exists so that, if this happens, you walk into the cesarean with planning, not just resignation.

```
IF MY TOLAC CONVERTS TO A CESAREAN

   WHAT I WANT IN THE OR

   ☐ Partner in the room (confirm hospital policy)
   ☐ My phone or camera with the partner for photos after
   ☐ Skin-to-skin with the baby in the OR, if possible
   ☐ Delayed cord clamping if it is safe
   ☐ The clear drape, if that is offered, so I can see them being born
   ☐ Quiet voices during the surgery
   ☐ Music if available (specific song or playlist:
       _____________________________________________)

   WHAT I WANT TO KNOW IN PLAIN LANGUAGE

   - Why the change is happening
   - How urgent it is
   - What to expect during the surgery

   THE EMOTIONAL PART

   You may need a day or a week or a year to process this. Use the
   P09 Postpartum Anxiety + PPD Journal if processing gets stuck.

   AFTER

   P06 C-Section Recovery Planner covers the next 5 weeks.

   ONE LINE TO YOURSELF, IN ADVANCE:

   _______________________________________________
   _______________________________________________
```

## Partner page

For the support person.

This is a different kind of birth planning than first-time pregnancies. The previous cesarean is a real event in your shared history. The decision now carries that weight.

```
THINGS THE SUPPORT PERSON CAN DO

DURING THE PREGNANCY

   ☐ Read the previous operative report with your partner
   ☐ Come to the provider conversations
   ☐ Listen to your partner's fears without trying to fix them
   ☐ Notice if your partner is carrying trauma from the first birth
     and gently suggest a therapist if so
   ☐ Help track the decision over time without pushing toward
     either outcome
   ☐ Do not have a strong personal opinion, or if you do, name it
     once and then let your partner lead

DURING LABOR (FOR A TOLAC)

   ☐ Bring the planner
   ☐ Be the person who asks "can you explain that in plain
     language" if something is unclear
   ☐ Watch your partner's energy, not just the monitors
   ☐ Be ready to advocate, if needed, for the things in the
     "if labor starts" page

IF IT CONVERTS

   ☐ Go with your partner to the OR
   ☐ Be the calm voice
   ☐ Take photos after the baby is here
   ☐ Be the person who fills in the rest of the planner
     because your partner will be recovering

IF IT IS A SCHEDULED REPEAT CESAREAN

   ☐ Drive your partner
   ☐ Be in the OR
   ☐ The post-op routine is in P06 C-Section Recovery Planner
   ☐ The first day home is mostly you doing the things your
     partner cannot do yet

THINGS TO SAY

   - "Whatever we decide is fine with me."
   - "I am with you, either way."
   - "What do you want to ask the provider next time?"
   - "How are you, separate from the decision?"

THINGS NOT TO SAY

   - "Just do whatever the provider says."
   - "I think you should ____."
   - "Your friend's VBAC went great so you should ____."
   - "It would be easier if we just ____."
```

## A final letter

You are going to have a baby. The route they get here is one of two routes. Both are legitimate. Both are real.

Whichever path this pregnancy ends with, it is not a measure of you. The previous cesarean was not a failure. A second cesarean would not be a failure. A successful TOLAC would not be a victory over the first birth. They are all just births.

What the planner cannot do is decide for you. What it can do is hold the questions, the conversations, the plans, and the trauma if there is any. So that on the day, whichever day it is, you walk in with the work already done.

You are going to meet them. The how is between you and your provider.

*Soothemade*
