You are becoming a parent. The route is one most planners do not cover.

The home study. The agency or attorney. The matching process. The waiting that has no due date. The phone call that may come this month or next year or never. The financial planning that runs alongside. The grief that is sometimes part of how you got here. The day they arrive, which may be the day they are born, or the day of a hearing, or the day a social worker drops them off with a duffel bag.

This planner is for that route. It is not a guide on how to adopt. The agency or attorney does that. It is the planning surface, the welcome-day plan, and the long-term language work that almost no resource does well.

## What's inside

Thirty pages, fillable:

- **A note before you start.** What this planner is.
- **"This is its own pregnancy" page.**
- **Home-study prep.** The big paperwork pull together.
- **Agency / lawyer / consultant tracker.**
- **Financial planning.** Costs + tax credits + grants.
- **The grief page.** Infertility loss, prior loss, birth-family complexity.
- **The wait, emotional check-ins.**
- **"When a match falls through" page.**
- **The match call.**
- **The welcome-day plan.**
- **The first week.**
- **Birth-family communication.**
- **"How we will talk about our family."**
- **A final letter.**

## Format

Instant-download PDF, US Letter + A4. Personal use only.
