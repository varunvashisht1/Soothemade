# P46 — Eldercare First 90 Days Planner

A 40-page planner for the adult child or family member who has just had a parent or partner's situation change significantly. The stroke, the fall, the dementia diagnosis, the hospital discharge to "now what." The first 90 days from the event when the new normal gets built.

## Why this exists

The Soothemade brand promise extends to "the eldercare years." This is the first product in that niche. The pregnancy + postpartum + early-years catalog is heavily served by 31 products. Eldercare gets nothing. This planner opens the niche on-brand.

The "first 90 days" is the window where the family figures out the new normal. Medical team. Financial and legal paperwork (POA, healthcare directive, estate). The "where will they live" decision. The siblings showing up or not showing up. The caregiver's own collapse.

## YMYL care

- YMYL careful. The planner does NOT name medications. It does not prescribe care decisions. All clinical questions route to the medical team. All financial decisions route to the financial planner or attorney.
- The "advance directive / POA" guidance is generic and routes to legal counsel.
- The "where will they live" page lists option types without endorsing any.
- The caregiver mental-health page lists crisis lines.

## Scope

Approximately 40 pages including:

- Cover, 3 front-matter pages
- The "first call list" page (day 1)
- Days 2-7 (week 1)
- Week 2-4 (first month closing)
- Week 5-8 (second month)
- Week 9-13 (third month closing)
- The medical team page (specialists, primary care, who is who)
- The medication log (by purpose only, no names)
- The financial + legal paperwork page (POA, advance directive, estate)
- The "where they will live" decision page
- The siblings + family page
- The "I am not okay either" caregiver burnout page
- Crisis lines
- The "what they want to remember" optional legacy page
- A final letter
