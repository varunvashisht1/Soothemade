# A Toddler Travel Activity Pack

*Thirty pages. The two-hour car ride. The four-hour flight. The bottom of hour three.*

---

## A note before you start

Travel with a toddler is a managed expectation.

You will not get the four hours of audiobook you used to get. You will not look out the window peacefully. You will spend the trip ten percent in your own head, ninety percent in whatever the kid is doing right now.

This pack is the printable bag of tricks. Scavenger hunts. I Spy. Conversation starters. Story prompts. Sing-along lyrics. A sticker chart. The "if they're losing it" page.

The pack is not magic. It will not turn a hard trip into an easy one. What it will do is give you ten minutes back at hour three, when you have run out of every other idea.

*With care, Maya*

> *This is a planning tool. For any travel-related medical questions, including motion sickness, ear pain on flights, or feeding considerations, please follow your pediatric provider's guidance. The pack does not name medications.*

---

## How to use this pack

- Print the whole pack. Pages are 8.5x11, easy to fold into a small envelope or folder.
- Bring colored markers or crayons in a separate small bag.
- Don't pull out everything at once. Rotate. One scavenger hunt, then a snack, then I Spy, then a song. Each thing buys you 10 to 20 minutes.
- The sticker chart goes in last, after the kid is fading. Stickers are a strong currency.

## Pre-trip prep checklist

```
ONE WEEK BEFORE

☐ Confirm car-seat installation, especially for long drives
☐ Buy or borrow any travel-specific gear (car-seat travel bag, lightweight
  stroller if flying, packable booster)
☐ Pack the activity bag (do not save this for the morning of)
☐ Charge any electronics
☐ Buy the snacks
☐ Buy the surprise wrapped toy
☐ Print this pack

THREE DAYS BEFORE

☐ Wash anything specific that needs to come (specific stuffed animal,
  specific blanket)
☐ Refill any prescription medications (call your provider for any
  specific guidance about traveling, ear pain on flights, motion
  sickness, etc.)
☐ Confirm where you are sleeping at the destination

THE NIGHT BEFORE

☐ Pack the snack bag (refrigerated items will go in tomorrow morning)
☐ Lay out the activity bag where you can grab it
☐ Lay out clothes for everyone for the morning
☐ Phone fully charged
☐ Reasonable bedtime for the kid (do not let them stay up "to be tired
  for the trip", overtired kids are harder, not easier)
```

---

## Day-of prep

```
MORNING OF

☐ Normal-ish breakfast (do not switch this morning)
☐ Bathroom right before leaving (and again before getting in the car / 
  onto the plane)
☐ Comfortable clothes (no scratchy, no new-shoes-first-time)
☐ Get the kid into the car / onto the plane awake if you can, so they
  can fall asleep there naturally rather than being transferred asleep
  (which often wakes them up)

FOR FLIGHTS SPECIFICALLY

☐ Give yourself extra time at security (toddler shoes off, water and
  food declared, stroller through scanner)
☐ Let them walk in the airport before boarding (the gate-area walk
  burns energy)
☐ Bring something to chew / drink / pacifier for takeoff and landing
  (helps with ear pressure)
☐ Pre-board if they offer family pre-boarding (less crowded)

FOR ROAD TRIPS SPECIFICALLY

☐ Plan rest stops every 1.5 to 2 hours, not "when we need to"
☐ Bring a towel and a yoga mat or blanket for impromptu nap spots
☐ Bring extra napkins, more than you think
☐ Have one parent ride in the back periodically if there are two of you
```

## Conversation starters

For when the kid is bored and the parent is also bored.

```
EASY ONES

- If our car could fly, where would we go?
- If you could only eat one food forever, what would you pick?
- What is the funniest thing you have ever seen?
- What is the best smell in the world?
- What sound do you like the most?

A LITTLE DEEPER

- What is something you are really good at?
- What was your favorite part of yesterday?
- If our family had a song, what would it be?
- What do you think dogs are saying when they bark?
- What do you think Grandma was like when she was little?

GAMES THAT KEEP GOING

- Twenty questions (kid picks an object, parent guesses with yes/no
  questions, then switch)
- Would you rather (alternate offering two choices)
- Story chain (each person adds one sentence to a story)
- Rhyme chain (each person says a word that rhymes with the last)
- "What if" (each person poses a hypothetical, the next answers it)
```

## If they are losing it

Hour three. Two-year-old is past the activity bag, past the snack, past the song. Crying. Yelling. Saying they want to get out.

```
THE SOOTHE SEQUENCE

1. Acknowledge.
   "I know. This has been a long trip. I'm tired too."

2. Validate the body.
   "Your body needs to move."

3. Adjust if possible.
   - If in the car: take the next exit, even if it is sooner than planned.
     Walk around a parking lot for 10 minutes.
   - If on the plane: get them out of the seat. Walk up and down the aisle
     if the seatbelt sign is off. Stand in the back near the bathroom.
   - If neither is possible: hold them on your lap. Sing softly. Let them
     cry. Crying is a release.

4. Reset.
   - Offer the wrapped surprise toy.
   - Offer a snack.
   - Offer to start a fresh activity from the bag.
   - Offer to watch something if you have headphones.

5. Survive.
   - This will pass.
   - You did not cause this.
   - The other passengers are not as upset as you think they are.
   - The kid will sleep eventually.

A NOTE TO YOURSELF, IN ADVANCE, FOR THE MOMENT YOU ARE EMBARRASSED:

"I am doing the best I can. The people around me have all been here.
The ones who have not been here are not the ones whose opinions I
care about. The trip will end. The kid will be fine. I will be fine."
```

## A final note

You traveled with a toddler. That alone is a thing.

You probably did not have the trip you were imagining. You probably had a different trip. The trip that included a meltdown at the airport gate. The trip that included the kid finally sleeping in the back row. The trip that included the moment they pointed out the window at something you had stopped seeing.

The pack carried you for a few of those hours. The rest was you.

You did the thing. You are home.
