# P38 — Postpartum Freezer Meal & One-Handed Cookbook

A small cookbook of 30 freezer-meal and one-handed-eating recipes for the postpartum stretch. With pre-cook-day grocery lists, kitchen-organization guidance, a freezer map, and a reheating reference.

## Why this exists

Postpartum food is its own problem. The parent doing the most eating cannot use two hands. The partner does not know what to make. The friends bringing meals do not know what helps. The internet recipe assumes you can stir a pot while standing for 25 minutes, which you cannot.

This cookbook is built around the actual constraint: one hand, twenty minutes, and a freezer that needs to do the cooking work the day-of-self cannot.

## YMYL care

- No allergen prescriptions. Each recipe has a "swap notes" line that names common adjustments but does not endorse them medically.
- No medication-related guidance ("eat this for milk supply" type claims are absent).
- A general "food allergies in the household are between you and your provider" note.

## Scope

Approximately 24 pages including:

- Cover, parent note, how-to-use, the "one-handed eating" principle
- Pre-cook day: grocery list templates (one per category for a cook-marathon)
- Pre-cook day: kitchen orientation + equipment minimums
- Five recipe categories with 30 total recipes:
  - Soups + stews (6 recipes)
  - Curries + one-pot bowls (6 recipes)
  - Casseroles + bakes (6 recipes)
  - Breakfast + make-ahead (6 recipes)
  - Snacks + smoothies + small things (6 recipes)
- Freezer organization map
- Reheating reference table
- A final note
