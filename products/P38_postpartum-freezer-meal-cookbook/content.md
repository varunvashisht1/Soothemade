# Postpartum Freezer Meal Cookbook

*Thirty recipes for the season when your hands are full. Make ahead. Reheat. Eat with one hand. Survive.*

---

## A note from the maker

The first six weeks of postpartum are not a season for ambitious cooking.

By the time you have figured out how to nurse without using your dominant hand, how to burp without putting the kid down, how to pee while holding the baby because they cry when you set them down, you do not have the bandwidth to dice an onion.

This cookbook is for that season. Every recipe is one-handed compatible. Every recipe is freezer-friendly. Every recipe is a partner's "what do I make tonight" answered before the question.

The pre-cook day is a real thing. Not the day before delivery, you will be too pregnant. Two weeks before is the right window. Or your partner does it the day after delivery while you are still at the hospital. Or your mother does it when she visits. Or a meal-train friend does it.

What you eat in the postpartum window matters. Not because you have to lose weight (you do not). Not because there are foods that make milk (the body does that). But because eating well makes a tired person less tired. Eating well makes a sad person less sad. Eating well is one of the cheapest things you can do for yourself, in a season when expensive things are not available.

*Soothemade*

> *This is a cookbook, not medical advice. Allergies and dietary restrictions in your household are between you and your provider. Adjust freely.*

---

## How to use this cookbook

- Print, or keep on a tablet. Some pages will get marked up.
- The pre-cook-day grocery list is at the front. Use it once. Reuse later.
- Recipes are grouped by category. Pick three or four from each category for a balanced freezer.
- Each recipe has rough yield, prep time, total time, freezer life, and the "one-handed verdict" (can you eat this with a fork, with the baby on the boob, at 11pm).
- Swap notes are on every recipe. Adapt freely. The recipe is a starting place.

---

## The one-handed eating principle

What does "one-handed compatible" mean.

It means:

- A fork, not a knife.
- A spoon for soft food, not a pair of chopsticks.
- Food you can lift to your mouth without dripping on the baby.
- Food that is good warm but also tolerable lukewarm, because you will not finish it before it cools.
- Food that does not require active stirring or attention while you eat.

It does not mean:

- Soggy.
- Tasteless.
- Stuff that comes in a foil packet.

The constraint is the hand. The food can still be good.

A small rule: every postpartum recipe should be edible at three temperatures: hot (the partner just made it), warm (you got to it within 20 minutes), and lukewarm (you got distracted). Soups pass. Stir-fries don't always.

## Kitchen orientation for the cooking day

What you need. What you do not need.

**Earns its space:**

- One large pot (5 to 7 quart). Soups, stews, curries.
- One large skillet (12 inch). Onions, ground meat, sauteed vegetables.
- One sheet pan (rimmed). Roasting batches.
- A good sharp knife. One is enough.
- A cutting board.
- A pressure cooker or slow cooker (either, not both required). Lifesaver.
- A blender or food processor. For smoothies and purees.
- A wooden spoon. A spatula. A ladle.
- Measuring cups + spoons.

**Skip:**

- Any specialty appliance you do not already own.
- Anything you have to assemble.
- Anything that takes more counter space than your usable counter.

**Labels matter.** Every container or bag goes in the freezer with:

- What it is (so future-you does not have to guess)
- Date frozen
- How to reheat in one sentence

A masking-tape label + Sharpie is enough.

## Soups + stews

The first 48 hours of postpartum are often best handled with broth-based food. Easy to reheat. Easy to eat lying down. Easy on a tender system.

## 1. White chicken chili

*Hearty, mild, freezer-friendly. Indian and Latin pantry-compatible.*

**Yield:** 8 servings    **Prep:** 15 min    **Total:** 1 hour    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, in a bowl with a spoon

**Ingredients:**

- 2 lb chicken thighs (boneless)
- 2 cans white beans (great northern), drained
- 1 large yellow onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cans diced green chilies
- 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp oregano, 1 tsp coriander
- 1 quart chicken broth
- Salt + pepper
- 1 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt (added when reheating, not freezing)

**Method:**

1. Saute onion + garlic in pot until soft.
2. Add chicken, broth, spices, beans, chilies. Simmer 30 min.
3. Shred chicken with two forks in the pot.
4. Cool. Portion into containers. Freeze.
5. To reheat: thaw overnight or use defrost. Warm in microwave. Stir in yogurt or sour cream just before eating.

**Swap notes:** Vegetarian version, swap chicken for 2 cans of chickpeas + 1 cup mushrooms. Dairy-free, skip the yogurt or use coconut cream.

## 2. Lentil-vegetable soup

*The "I have no energy" soup. Vegan if you want.*

**Yield:** 8 servings    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 45 min    **Freezer life:** 4 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 2 cups red lentils
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 carrots, diced
- 3 celery stalks, diced
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 can crushed tomatoes
- 2 quarts broth (vegetable or chicken)
- 2 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander
- Juice of 1 lemon (added at reheating)
- Salt + pepper

**Method:**

1. Saute onion, carrot, celery in pot 5 min.
2. Add garlic, lentils, tomatoes, broth, spices. Simmer 25 min.
3. Cool. Portion. Freeze flat in bags.
4. To reheat: warm in microwave or stovetop. Stir in lemon juice at the end.

**Swap notes:** Indian version, add 1 tsp garam masala + a piece of fresh ginger. Topped with cilantro and yogurt when serving.

## 3. Beef + barley stew

*Hearty, sticks-to-your-bones. The 11pm partner meal.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 20 min    **Total:** 90 min in slow cooker    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, with a spoon

**Ingredients:**

- 1.5 lb stew beef, cubed
- 1 cup pearl barley
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 carrots, sliced
- 3 celery stalks, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 quart beef broth
- 1 can diced tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 bay leaves, salt + pepper

**Method:**

1. Brown beef in pot.
2. Add everything else.
3. Slow cooker 6 hours on low, or stovetop simmer 90 min.
4. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
5. To reheat: thaw, warm slowly. The barley will absorb liquid, add water or broth.

**Swap notes:** Use ground beef if you do not want to cube. Vegetarian, swap beef for mushrooms + lentils.

## 4. Tomato-coconut soup

*Creamy without dairy. Indian-comforting.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 30 min    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 2 cans crushed tomatoes
- 1 can full-fat coconut milk
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 inch ginger, grated
- 2 tsp curry powder, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander
- 1 cup broth
- Salt + pepper
- Cilantro for garnish (not frozen)

**Method:**

1. Saute onion, garlic, ginger.
2. Add tomatoes, coconut milk, spices, broth. Simmer 20 min.
3. Blend with immersion blender for smooth, or leave chunky.
4. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
5. Reheat slowly so coconut milk does not separate.

**Swap notes:** Add a can of chickpeas for protein. Stir in spinach at reheating.

## 5. Chicken-rice porridge (congee or khichdi-style)

*Soft, easy on a recovering system. Comfort food.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 1 hour    **Freezer life:** 2 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, very

**Ingredients:**

- 1 cup brown rice
- 1 lb chicken thighs
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 inch ginger, grated
- 2 quarts broth
- 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp salt
- Soy sauce + green onions to garnish

**Method:**

1. Combine rice, chicken, onion, garlic, ginger, broth, spices in pot.
2. Simmer 1 hour until rice has broken down into porridge consistency.
3. Shred chicken in the pot.
4. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
5. Reheat with extra broth or water (it will thicken on standing).

**Swap notes:** South Asian khichdi-style, swap chicken for moong dal and add a pinch of asafoetida. Garnish with ghee.

## 6. Black bean + sweet potato chili

*Vegetarian, freezer queen, the friend's drop-off meal.*

**Yield:** 8 servings    **Prep:** 15 min    **Total:** 45 min    **Freezer life:** 4 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 3 cans black beans, drained
- 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 can crushed tomatoes
- 1 can chipotle in adobo (use 1 to 2 peppers, mince)
- 2 tsp cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp coriander
- 1 quart broth
- Juice of 1 lime (at reheating)
- Salt + pepper

**Method:**

1. Saute onion + garlic.
2. Add sweet potato, beans, tomato, chipotle, broth, spices.
3. Simmer 30 min, until sweet potato is tender.
4. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
5. Reheat, finish with lime.

**Swap notes:** Add ground turkey or ground beef for non-vegetarian. Skip chipotle if you cannot do spicy postpartum.

## 7. Coconut chickpea curry

*Pantry curry. No fresh ingredients required.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 35 min    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, over rice

**Ingredients:**

- 2 cans chickpeas, drained
- 1 can crushed tomatoes
- 1 can full-fat coconut milk
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch ginger, grated
- 1 tbsp curry powder, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 cup broth
- Salt
- Cilantro for garnish at serving

**Method:**

1. Saute onion, garlic, ginger.
2. Add spices, toast 30 seconds.
3. Add tomatoes, coconut milk, chickpeas, broth. Simmer 25 min.
4. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
5. Reheat. Serve over rice.

**Swap notes:** Add spinach at the end for greens. Add cubed chicken thighs at step 1 for protein.

## 8. Lentil dal

*The "I miss home" food.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 40 min    **Freezer life:** 4 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, over rice

**Ingredients:**

- 1.5 cups red lentils (masoor dal)
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch ginger, grated
- 2 tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can diced)
- 2 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp red chili powder
- 6 cups water or broth
- 2 tbsp ghee or oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 dried red chili (for tempering, optional)
- Cilantro at serving

**Method:**

1. Rinse lentils. Combine with onion, tomato, spices, water. Simmer 30 min.
2. Heat ghee separately. Add cumin seeds + chili until fragrant. Pour over dal.
3. Cool. Portion. Freeze.
4. Reheat with a splash of water (it thickens).

**Swap notes:** Vegan, use oil instead of ghee. Add spinach at reheating.

## 13. Baked ziti

*The friend's drop-off meal. Reliable as gravity.*

**Yield:** 8 servings    **Prep:** 25 min    **Total:** 1 hour    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, with a fork

**Ingredients:**

- 1 lb ziti or penne
- 2 jars marinara sauce
- 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 2 cups ricotta cheese
- 2 cups mozzarella, shredded
- 1/2 cup parmesan
- 1 egg
- Italian herbs

**Method:**

1. Cook pasta al dente. Drain.
2. Brown meat with onion + garlic.
3. Add marinara to meat.
4. Mix ricotta + egg.
5. Layer in baking dish: meat sauce, pasta, ricotta mixture, mozzarella. Repeat.
6. Top with parmesan.
7. Cool. Wrap tightly. Freeze whole, or portion into containers.
8. Bake from frozen at 375 for 45 min covered, then 15 min uncovered.

**Swap notes:** Vegetarian, skip meat, add roasted vegetables. Gluten-free pasta works.

## 14. Chicken + rice casserole

*Slightly retro, perfect comfort.*

**Yield:** 6 servings    **Prep:** 15 min    **Total:** 1 hour    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 1.5 lb chicken thighs
- 1.5 cups rice
- 3 cups broth
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
- 1 can cream-of-mushroom-style soup
- 1 cup shredded cheese
- Salt + pepper, paprika

**Method:**

1. In baking dish, combine rice, broth, soup, onion, vegetables, spices.
2. Lay chicken on top.
3. Cover. Bake at 375 for 50 min.
4. Uncover, top with cheese, bake 10 more min.
5. Cool. Portion. Freeze.

**Swap notes:** Use cauliflower rice instead of rice (less liquid).

## 15. Lasagna with hidden vegetables

*Classic. Hides spinach in plain sight.*

**Yield:** 10 servings    **Prep:** 40 min    **Total:** 1.5 hours    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 12 lasagna noodles
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 2 jars marinara
- 1 box frozen spinach, thawed + drained
- 2 cups ricotta
- 3 cups mozzarella
- 1/2 cup parmesan
- 1 egg

**Method:**

1. Cook noodles to just-soft.
2. Brown meat with onion + garlic. Add marinara.
3. Mix ricotta + egg + spinach.
4. Layer in 9x13: sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella. Repeat.
5. Top with parmesan.
6. Wrap tightly. Freeze.
7. Bake from frozen at 375, 60-75 min covered, then 15 min uncovered.

**Swap notes:** Vegetarian, skip the meat and add layers of roasted zucchini.

## 18. Breakfast strata (egg + bread bake)

## 19. Steel-cut overnight oats (one pot, a week of breakfasts)

*Make Sunday night. Eat Monday through Friday.*

**Yield:** 5 servings    **Prep:** 5 min    **Total:** overnight    **Freezer life:** 1 month    **One-handed verdict:** yes, with a spoon

**Ingredients:**

- 1.5 cups steel-cut oats
- 6 cups water
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- Toppings (added at eating, not freezing): nut butter, banana, berries, milk, honey

**Method:**

1. Bring water + salt + cinnamon to boil.
2. Add oats. Reduce to low. Cook 25 min.
3. Cool. Portion into 5 jars or containers.
4. Refrigerate up to 5 days. Or freeze for up to a month.
5. Reheat with a splash of milk in microwave. Top.

**Swap notes:** Make savory, swap cinnamon for salt + pepper, top with egg + greens.

## 20. Make-ahead breakfast burritos

*The 9am-after-night-shift meal.*

**Yield:** 10 burritos    **Prep:** 30 min    **Total:** 45 min    **Freezer life:** 2 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, the whole point

**Ingredients:**

- 10 large flour tortillas
- 12 eggs, scrambled
- 1 lb sausage or 2 cups roasted sweet potato cubes
- 2 cups shredded cheese
- 1 can black beans, drained
- Salsa

**Method:**

1. Scramble eggs.
2. Cook sausage or roast sweet potato.
3. Lay out tortillas. Fill each with eggs, sausage/potato, beans, cheese, salsa.
4. Roll, tucking in sides.
5. Wrap each in parchment + foil.
6. Freeze in a gallon bag.
7. To reheat: microwave 2 min, then 1 min more. Or oven from frozen 25 min at 375.

**Swap notes:** Vegetarian filling, skip sausage. Hot sauce + avocado at eating.

## 22. Banana-oat-nut-butter freezer pancakes

*Make a big batch. Reheat in toaster.*

**Yield:** 16 pancakes    **Prep:** 10 min    **Total:** 30 min    **Freezer life:** 2 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 2 cups rolled oats, blended into flour
- 2 ripe bananas
- 4 eggs
- 1/2 cup nut butter
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1 tsp baking powder, 1 tsp cinnamon, pinch of salt
- Optional, chocolate chips or berries

**Method:**

1. Blend everything until smooth.

3. Cool completely.
4. Stack with parchment between pancakes. Freeze in gallon bag.
5. Reheat in toaster.

**Swap notes:** Skip eggs for vegan version, add 1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water per egg.

## 25. Lactation-friendly oatmeal cookies (no claims, just good)

*We do not claim they boost supply. We do claim they are delicious and easy.*

**Yield:** 24 cookies    **Prep:** 15 min    **Total:** 30 min    **Freezer life:** 2 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes, the most yes

**Ingredients:**

- 1 cup butter, softened
- 3/4 cup brown sugar
- 1/2 cup white sugar
- 2 eggs
- 1 tsp vanilla
- 2 cups rolled oats
- 2 cups flour
- 1 cup chocolate chips
- 1/4 cup ground flax
- 2 tbsp brewer's yeast (optional)
- 1 tsp baking soda, 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp cinnamon

**Method:**

1. Cream butter + sugars.
2. Add eggs + vanilla.
3. Stir in dry ingredients.
4. Bake at 350 for 12 min.
5. Cool. Freeze in a gallon bag.

**Swap notes:** Skip brewer's yeast and ground flax for regular oatmeal cookies. Swap chocolate chips for raisins or dried cranberries.

## 27. Hardboiled eggs (the 3am protein)

*Not glamorous. Saves your life at 3am.*

**Yield:** 12    **Prep:** 5 min    **Total:** 15 min    **Refrigerator life:** 1 week    **One-handed verdict:** depends, more like one and a half hands

**Method:**

1. Place eggs in pot. Cover with cold water.
2. Bring to boil. Remove from heat. Cover. Let sit 12 min.
3. Drain. Ice bath. Peel.
4. Store peeled in fridge.

A reminder: keep them peeled and visible at eye level in the fridge so you actually eat them.

## 28. Banana-bread loaves (mini)

*Mini loaves freeze better than one big loaf.*

**Yield:** 4 mini loaves    **Prep:** 15 min    **Total:** 45 min    **Freezer life:** 3 months    **One-handed verdict:** yes

**Ingredients:**

- 4 ripe bananas
- 2 eggs
- 1/3 cup melted butter or oil
- 1/2 cup brown sugar
- 1/2 cup yogurt
- 2 cups flour
- 1 tsp baking soda, 1 tsp baking powder, pinch salt
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- Optional, chocolate chips or walnuts

**Method:**

1. Mash bananas. Whisk in eggs, butter, sugar, yogurt.
2. Stir in dry ingredients.
3. Pour into 4 mini loaf pans (greased).
4. Bake at 350 for 30 min.
5. Cool. Wrap individually. Freeze.

**Swap notes:** Add zucchini for hidden vegetable. Use coconut oil for dairy-free.
