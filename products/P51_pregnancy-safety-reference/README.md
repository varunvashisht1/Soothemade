# P51 — The Pregnancy Safety Reference

A 30-page reference for the things pregnant people commonly google. Food, drink, environment, activity, common over-the-counter situations. Organized by category so the parent can find what they need and bring questions to their provider, instead of falling into a 2am internet hole.

## Why this exists

Most pregnant people have a long, repeating list of small questions. "Can I have deli meat. Can I drink coffee. Is hot yoga okay. Can I dye my hair. What about cleaning the bathtub." The questions arrive scattered, often at night, often via Google, often via forum posts whose accuracy is a coin flip.

This reference does not answer the questions. It organizes them so the parent can ask a real provider in one conversation, instead of ten 2am Google searches.

## YMYL care

- This is one of the most YMYL-sensitive products in the catalog. The voice is **organized list, not safety verdict**.
- NO medication names (brand or generic).
- NO definitive "this is safe" or "this is dangerous" statements.
- NO specific dose limits or thresholds.
- NO vaccine recommendations or anti-recommendations.
- Every section ends with "this is your provider's call."
- A specific "if I already did this thing" page that frames past actions calmly without medical prediction.

## Scope

Approximately 30 pages including:

- Cover, 3 front-matter pages
- The big disclaimer page
- Food categories (the questions most people have)
- Drink categories
- Activity categories
- Environment categories (chemicals, paint, cat litter, etc.)
- Medication classes (no names)
- Workplace categories
- Travel categories
- "What they say but actually" plain-language overview page
- "If I already did this thing" calm-framework page
- The "this is for your provider" question-list template
- A final note
