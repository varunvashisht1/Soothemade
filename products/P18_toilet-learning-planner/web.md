Most toilet-learning guides will tell you it took their kid three days. It did not take their kid three days. It took their kid two months and they don't want to talk about week three.

This is a planner for the real thing. Which is: a stretch of two to twelve weeks where the days look uneven, the kid leads, the adult mostly cleans, and at the end of it something has shifted that you cannot un-shift.

## What's inside

Twenty-eight pages:

- **The readiness page**. Twelve signs (some physical, some behavioral, some social) that say roughly "the time is closer than further". You can wait. You can also start. There is no medal for being early.
- **The gear list**. Two of everything. Where to put the small potty. The step stool decision. Sheets. Mattress protector. Travel kit.
- **A four-week gentle path**. Not a system. Not a method. A pace.
- **The first three days log**. Wet/dry/poop, by half-hour. Not for scoring, for spotting the pattern. (The pattern usually emerges around day four or five.)
- **Accident-day scripts**. Six things to say. "It's okay. Bodies are learning." "Let's get clean clothes together." The scripts for not making your face do the face.
- **The daycare handoff page**. What they need to know. The signal your kid uses. The rituals you've built. The backup clothes.
- **The grandparent handoff page**. Slightly different from daycare. Includes the conversation about how this is NOT the way it was done with you.
- **The night phase**. Optional, separate, often months after daytime is solid. A short page on what's known about night dryness.
- **Regression expectations**. Number-one trigger is a new sibling. Number two is a move. Number three is starting school. What to do.
- **The "I'm more stressed than my kid" page**. Because that's most of us.

## Who this is for

You, with a toddler who has been hiding behind the couch to poop in their diaper, which is one of the readiness signs.

You, who has now hosted three other parents who got their kid into underwear and you feel behind.

You, on day four, who is not seeing the pattern yet. (Wait one more day.)

You, with twins. There's a page for you.

Not for: a child with toileting concerns flagged by a pediatric provider. Those require a real provider, not a planner.
