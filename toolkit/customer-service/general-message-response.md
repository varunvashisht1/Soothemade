# Customer Service — General Message Response

## How this file works

Templates below. Copy, fill in [BRACKETS], send. Most Etsy / Gumroad buyer messages fall into 10 categories. Pick the right one.

## Template 01 — "Can I customize this?"

```
Hi [name]!

Thanks for thinking of this for [their context]. Just to be honest
up-front — this is a finished PDF, not an editable template, so I
can't customize it directly.

That said: most of my buyers print and write directly on it, or use
a PDF annotation app to mark it up. If you're tech-comfortable, the
PDF is editable in Adobe Acrobat (paid) or Foxit Reader (free).

If there's a specific change you'd want, write back and tell me —
I keep a list of customer requests and a few of them have become
new products. No promises, but I read every one.

— [persona]
```

## Template 02 — "Where's my download?"

can't find it, reply with your order number and I'll send the
files directly.

## Tone rules (apply to all replies)

- Reply in under 4 sentences when possible.
- Match their warmth. If they were terse, keep it terse. If they were warm, return it.
- Sign off in voice. "— [persona]" works.
- Never apologize 3 times in one message. Once max.
- Never get defensive. If they're rude, be neutral.
- Refund requests: always say yes, no questions, no upsell.
- Genuine thanks: respond once, warmly, then move on. Don't ask for the review unless they had a great experience.

## When to escalate

These messages need owner judgment, not template:

- Legal threats / DMCA / copyright claims
- Medical claims (someone using a product in a way that backfires)
- Repeated harassment from one buyer
- Press / media inquiries
- Wholesale offers over $500
- Anything that mentions a child's safety incident

For these: don't reply yet. Sit overnight. Then reply carefully.
