# Customer Service — Negative Review Response

## The principle

A negative review isn't a fire. It's a public conversation watched by your future buyers. The reply is more important than the review.

Future buyers are mostly looking for: "does this seller act like a reasonable person under pressure?" If yes, the negative review barely matters.

## The 4-line response template

```
Hi [name],

I'm sorry [the product didn't work for you / the specific thing
they said didn't work]. [Acknowledge their specific point — show
you read the review].

[Optional: one sentence on what you've learned or changed.]

If a refund would help, just message me. I want this to be a fair
transaction for both of us.

— [persona]
```

That's it. Four lines.

## Examples in practice

### Negative review: "The PDF was just text. I expected images."

```
Hi [name],

That's fair — the listing could be clearer that this is a
text-based resource. I'll update the description to make that
upfront.

If a refund would help, just message me.

— [persona]
```

### Negative review: "Disappointed. Not what I expected for $14."

```
Hi [name],

Sorry it didn't land for you. If it wasn't right, refund is no
problem — just message me. New parenthood is expensive enough
without buying things that don't help.

— [persona]
```

### Negative review: "The advice in here is wrong/dangerous."

(This is the most serious type — it's also rare.)

```
Hi [name],

Thank you for flagging this. I take medical-safety concerns
seriously — could you point me to which page or which specific
guidance you'd revise? I'll review it carefully and update if
needed.

If you'd prefer to refund instead, of course — just say the word.

— [persona]
```

(Then actually review it. Pull the listing if a real safety concern exists. Update content. Re-publish.)

### Negative review: "This is just AI/generic content."

```
Hi [name],

That's fair feedback. I worked with AI tools to organize and
draft this, then edited heavily — it's an honest hybrid. If that's
not what you wanted, totally understandable. Refund is no problem,
just message me.

— [persona]
```

(Then think about whether the product is, in fact, generic. If yes — fix it.)

### Negative review: "Took too long to download." or "Etsy was confusing."

```
Hi [name],

That's frustrating. The PDF is available right after purchase in
your "Purchases & Reviews" tab — if it didn't come through, message
me and I'll send it directly.

— [persona]
```

(Etsy delivery issues aren't your fault but you wear them.)

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't argue with the reviewer publicly. Even if they're objectively wrong.
- ❌ Don't get defensive. "I clearly stated in the listing..." reads badly to everyone reading it.
- ❌ Don't beg them to update the review. Just be reasonable; if they want to update, they will.
- ❌ Don't try to be funny or sarcastic. Reads cold.
- ❌ Don't write more than 4 lines. Length signals desperation.
- ❌ Don't apologize 3 times. Once is sincerity. Three is groveling.
- ❌ Don't promise specific feature changes ("I'll add X next week!") unless you really will.

## When to ask Etsy to remove a review

Almost never. Etsy will only remove reviews that:
- Violate their content policy (profanity, personal attacks)
- Are blackmail (e.g., "give me a free X or I'll leave a 1-star")
- Are about shipping issues outside your control (digital products N/A here)

Don't try to remove "honest negative" reviews. That's a losing battle and looks bad if Etsy denies.

## After a negative review hits

1. **Respond within 24 hours.** Speed signals "I read this."
2. **Issue refund if requested.** Don't haggle.
3. **Note the feedback in `publishing/metrics.md`.** Track patterns.
4. **If 3+ reviews mention the same issue, the issue is real.** Fix the product.
5. **Move on.** A single negative review with a calm 4-line reply doesn't tank a listing. Continued obsessing about it does.

## The compound rule

Future buyers read your responses to negative reviews more carefully than your positive reviews. Make every response a quiet demonstration of: I'm a reasonable person, I care, and I won't be defensive when something's off.

That alone converts skeptics into buyers.
