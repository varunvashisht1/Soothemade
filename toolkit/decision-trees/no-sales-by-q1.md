# Decision Tree — No Sales by Week 12

> Use this if you're 12 weeks in and have fewer than 3 sales total. Don't panic. Triage in this order.

## Step 1 — Diagnose the bottleneck

Pull these numbers from Etsy stats:

| Numbers | Diagnosis |
|---|---|
| <50 views per listing in 90 days | Listings aren't ranking. SEO issue. |
| 50–200 views, 0 favorites | Photos / titles aren't compelling. CTR issue. |
| 200+ views, 5+ favorites, 0 sales | Price / trust / product fit. Conversion issue. |
| 500+ views per listing, multiple favorites, still 0 sales | Niche-fit issue. Product wrong. |

## Step 2 — Run the matching playbook

### Playbook A — SEO issue (no views)

Run for top 3 listings:

1. Re-do listing titles using **Prompt 02 — Etsy SEO refresh**
2. Compare title structure to current top 3 sellers in the category (search Etsy, note the patterns — long descriptive vs short keyword-stuffed)
3. Re-do tags: replace 4–5 of the lowest-impression tags with rising Pinterest terms
4. Verify category and attributes are filled out completely
5. Wait 2 weeks. Re-pull stats.

If after 2 more weeks still <100 views: this listing's market is dead. Kill it.

### Playbook B — CTR issue (views but no favorites)

1. Replace listing photo #1 — this is the single biggest CTR lever
2. New thumbnail mockup: try one of: (a) printed-on-table flatlay, (b) iPad mockup, (c) bold text-on-color
3. Verify your first photo is *not* a generic stock image with text on top — those underperform now
4. Test for 2 weeks
5. If still flat: the product itself isn't resonating; move to Playbook D

### Playbook C — Conversion issue (favorites but no sales)

1. Re-read the listing description with fresh eyes — is the first paragraph hooking specifically?
2. Test a price drop of $1–2 (sometimes pricing too low looks suspicious; sometimes too high abandons cart — A/B by changing once and waiting)
3. Add a recent review screenshot to your photo set if you have any
4. Check that "Auto-renew" is on (listings expire silently otherwise)
5. Look at the actual full listing as a buyer would on mobile

If still 0 after 4 weeks: trust signal issue. You may be too new — let time build it.

### Playbook D — Niche-fit issue (lots of activity, no sales)

This is the hardest. Means the buyers ARE there, they ARE interested, they're not buying. Reasons:

- They expected something the product doesn't deliver
- Price-to-perceived-value mismatch
- Competing free alternatives are too easy
- The product is fundamentally wrong for the niche

Do this:

1. Run **Prompt 05 — Quarterly Review**
2. Honestly ask: would *you* buy this if you were the buyer?
3. Pick your top 3 underperformers and *kill them*. Free up the slots.
4. Look at your top 1 (even if it's only 1 sale): can you make 3 variants of that?
5. Refocus on the variant strategy, not new categories

## Step 3 — The "pivot or persist" question

By **Week 13** (Q1 review):
- If your best product has any sales at all → **persist**. Stay the course. Etsy's algorithm rewards consistency.
- If 0 sales total → **review the niche fit**. Did the research point to specific underserved segments you ignored? Did you launch wrong category orders?

By **Week 26** (Q2 review):
- If <$200 total revenue → **niche pivot is appropriate**. Look at your traffic data: which products got the most Pinterest impressions even without sales? Pivot toward those topics.
- If $200–$1000 → **continue with refinement**. You have signal; double down.

By **Week 39** (Q3 review):
- If <$500 total → **wind-down evaluation**. The shop may not be a fit for you. There's no shame in this. Wind down or rebrand with new niche.
- If $500–$2000 → **steady refinement, no panic**.

By **Week 52** (Year-end):
- If <$1000 → **honest reset**. Either pivot hard or close.
- If $1000–$5000 → **you have a side income**. Decide if you want to invest more time to grow it.
- If $5000+ → **you have a real business**. Time to think about a bigger website, paid help, or just enjoy the slow growth.

## What is NOT a reason to panic

- 0 sales in week 1 — Etsy's algorithm indexes new shops over weeks
- 0 sales in weeks 1–4 — totally normal, base rate
- One bad review — see `negative-review.md`, move on
- A competitor launching similar — there's room for many sellers
- Pinterest pin gets one viral hit, then nothing — totally normal traffic pattern
- A friend says "the design is ugly" — friends are not your buyer (usually)

## What IS worth panicking about

- A product flagged by Etsy for medical/safety violation → fix and re-list, don't ignore
- A pattern of negative reviews around the same issue → fix the product
- Etsy or Gumroad account suspended → respond to their requests immediately
- A buyer's-message about a real safety concern → take overnight, respond carefully

## The honest closing note

If you've genuinely tried — built thoughtful products, listed them well, dripped consistently for 90 days — and there's no signal: that's data. Not a personal failure.

Some niches are saturated. Some aesthetic-trend bets don't pay off. Some shops just don't click with their audience.

Wind down with grace. Refund any in-flight requests, archive the assets to a folder, take the learnings, and move on. The work you did is portable — voice, brand sense, the Pro-mode prompt library, the publishing system. All of it transfers to whatever you build next.
