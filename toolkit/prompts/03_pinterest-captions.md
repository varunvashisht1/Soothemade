# Prompt 03 — Pinterest pin captions

## Use when

You want 5 fresh pin captions for an existing product (refresh) or a new product.

## Time

3–5 min on Claude Pro

## Prompt (paste into Claude)

```
I need 5 Pinterest pin caption variants for a product in my new-parent
digital-products shop.

BRAND VOICE: A slightly-older friend who's been through it. Warm-direct,
never preachy. No "mama", "journey", "blessed", or hustle vocabulary.
Specific concrete observations beat generalities.

PRODUCT NAME: [Name]
WHAT IT IS (1 sentence): [Description]
WHO IT'S FOR: [Audience]
PRICE: $[X]

I want 5 pin captions, each with:
- A headline (5–9 words, designed for image overlay)
- A pin title (60–80 chars for Pinterest's title field)
- A pin description (1–2 sentences, ending in a soft CTA)

5 different angles:
1. The "specific pain moment" hook
2. The product-name + audience hook
3. A single-line quote pulled from the product itself
4. The "this isn't what you think" / anti-cliche hook
5. A surprising data or trend angle (use rising Pinterest term if possible)
```

## Expected output

5 mini pin specs. Each is ~30 words. Total output ~200 words. Easy on Pro.

## After you run this

1. Open Canva → make pins per `brand/visual-identity.md`
2. Schedule via Pinterest or upload manually
3. Tag pins with `?utm_source=pinterest&utm_campaign=PXX_caption_N` for tracking

## Pinterest caption rules

- **Headline (on image)** must be readable in 1 sec. Test at thumbnail size.
- **Pin title** is the SEO field — pack the keyword early
- **Pin description** is mostly invisible to most viewers but matters for SEO
- **Hashtags** are no longer supported on Pinterest — don't bother
- Avoid emoji — they hurt CTR on Pinterest currently
