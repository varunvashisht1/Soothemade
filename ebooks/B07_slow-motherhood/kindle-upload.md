**Description:**

```
A friend asked me what "slow motherhood" means.

It is not the Sunday in a sunbeam with a clean linen apron and a sourdough loaf.

It is the Tuesday at 4pm when the laundry is still in the dryer and the baby is finally asleep and you have to choose between sitting with your coffee for four minutes or starting dinner, and you pick the coffee.

That is slow motherhood. The four minutes you choose yourself, when there is an equally valid case for the laundry.

This book is permission, written down.

Inside: short chapters on what slow motherhood actually looks like in a real week, the optimization trap, the comparison trap, the "highlight reel" trap, what to do with the guilt that comes with picking yourself, and a closing essay on the version of motherhood Pinterest will never sell back to you.

Short. Honest. Read it in one evening. Pick it up again the next time someone makes you feel slow.

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula and mother of two. For the mom who is tired of being told she needs a system. She does not need a system. She needs four minutes.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography. Slow, considered companions for the parts of life no one is photographing.
```
