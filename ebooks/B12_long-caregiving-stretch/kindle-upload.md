**Series:** Soothemade · Book 12

**Author:** Soothemade

**Description:**

```
For the family caregiver who is no longer in the first 90 days.

The crisis has settled into routine. The hospital phone call is replaced by a slower kind of weight: the every-Tuesday medication refill, the every-Sunday Sunday lunch, the running tally of doctor appointments and bath days, the friends who stopped asking, the marriage that is straining, the career that has paused, the question you do not let yourself ask out loud — how long.

This book is the sister to Caregiving, the First 90 Days. Written for the long haul.

Inside:

• The respite-care planning chapter — named, in this book, as the most important chapter
• The "I am angry" page, with the usually-unwritten "angry at my loved one" sub-page
• The "I cannot do this forever" reckoning page
• A four-page sibling-conflict workbook
• Marriage and career considerations for the long stretch
• The "what they would want, if they could tell me" page
• Hospice considerations with the "hospice is not giving up" reframe
• Caregiver crisis lines: Family Caregiver Alliance (1-800-445-8106), Alzheimer's Association (1-800-272-3900), Eldercare Locator (1-800-677-1116), 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Written by Maya. Plain language. No "treasure these moments." No advice to "take time for yourself" without telling you how. Real frameworks for the long stretch.

If you are in crisis, please reach out to one of the crisis lines above. The book is not a substitute for support.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography. Pair with Caregiving, the First 90 Days (Book 11) — together they cover from the diagnosis through the long road that follows.
```
