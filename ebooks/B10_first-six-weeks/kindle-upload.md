**Description:**

```
The first six weeks home with a newborn are not the six weeks any other book is written for.

You will walk into the kitchen and forget what you came for. You will text your partner from the next room. You will set the bottle down on the dresser and find it three days later, in the laundry pile, mostly intact. You are not failing. You are running on three hours of sleep, broken into pieces, and your brain has chosen to keep you alive over remembering what you were doing.

This book is the scaffold for that brain.

Inside:

• A week-by-week structure for six weeks home — what to expect, what is normal, what is not
• Feed and diaper log templates you can actually fill in at 4am
• The "what we tried" log for the things that worked and the things that did not
• Visitor scripts for the people who keep showing up (cross-references B01)
• The 3am Google reference page — the questions you will want answered before you spiral
• Recovery check-ins for the parent who gave birth
• Pediatrician prep for the 2-week and 6-week visits
• The "I am not okay" page with postpartum mental-health crisis routing (PSI 1-833-852-6262)

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula. Not a methodology. Not a parenting philosophy. A scaffold for the kind of week where you cannot keep track of anything.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography. Pair with How to Read Your Baby (Book 3) and 30 Scripts for the Postpartum Visitor (Book 1).
```
