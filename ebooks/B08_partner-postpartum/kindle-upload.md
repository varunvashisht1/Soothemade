**Series:** Soothemade · Book 8

**Description:**

```
A guide written TO the non-birthing partner. Not to her. To you.

The first twelve weeks after a baby arrives are not the twelve weeks anyone prepares you for. You have read parenting books and they are written for the parent who gives birth. The "partner" section is usually four pages at the end that say "be supportive." This book is the four hundred pages that should have come first.

What to actually do. In what order. Week by week.

Inside:

• Hospital + homecoming chapter (what your job is at the bedside, at discharge, at the first night home)
• Twelve weekly chapters from week one through week twelve, each with what is happening for her, what is happening for the baby, and what is happening for you
• Special-situations chapters: c-section recovery, breastfeeding, bottle-feeding, difficult delivery, same-sex couple, return to work
• The "before the baby" conversation — the one you should have at week 36
• The things she will want to hear that she may never tell you to say
• The "page just for you" at the back, including the reminder that partner postpartum depression is real and underdiagnosed

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula. Plain language. No "be more present." Real scripts for real moments.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography.
```
