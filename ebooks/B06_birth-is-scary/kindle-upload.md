**Language:** English (United States)

**Book Title:**
> Birth Is Scary

**Subtitle:**
> A Field Guide to the Fear of Giving Birth. Validating Birth Anxiety, Naming the Specific Fears, and Scripts for the People Around You.

**Series:** Soothemade · Book 6

**Author:** Soothemade

**Description:**

```
You are scared of giving birth, and the people around you keep telling you to trust your body and that women have been doing this for thousands of years.

This book is for you anyway.

It treats birth fear as data, not weakness. It names the specific sub-fears that everyone has but no one says out loud: pain, loss of control, complications for you, complications for the baby, interventions you did not want, the unknown of the after. It walks you through each one slowly, with tools that are not platitudes.

Inside:

• Fear inventory: name yours from a list of ~50, in plain words
• Six two-page workbooks for the most common sub-fears
• Scripts for telling the team you are scared (the provider, the partner, the mother, the friend who keeps saying "but it's beautiful")
• The "night before labor" page — a letter from you, to you-in-labor
• The "I am not okay" page with perinatal mental-health crisis routing (PSI 1-833-852-6262, Maternal Mental Health Hotline 1-833-943-5746)

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula. Not a hypnobirth pamphlet. Not "release the fear." Just an honest companion for the fear that already exists, written by someone who believes the fear is reasonable.

Crisis support: if your anxiety is bigger than this book, please reach out to Postpartum Support International (1-833-852-6262 in the US) or your local equivalent. The book is not a substitute for care.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography.
```
