**Description:**

```
You are pregnant. You just ate the deli sandwich. Or drank the cold-brew. Or sat next to the smoker. Or noticed the cleaning supply you used yesterday is on the "ask your provider" list. And now it is 2am and you have 14 tabs open and none of them agree.

This book is the slow-paced alternative.

It does not make safety verdicts. It organizes the questions across the seven categories where pregnant people get them — food and drink, activity and exercise, environment and chemicals, medication classes by purpose, workplace exposures, travel, and the "I already did this thing, now what" framework — so you can write down what you want to ask the provider in one calm conversation, instead of ten frantic Google searches.

Inside:

• 7 chapters of questions to bring to your prenatal appointment
• The "what they say but actually" page deconstructing common internet patterns
• The "if I already did this thing" calm framework
• A question-list template you can print and hand to the provider
• The reminder, on every page, that the answers belong to the provider, not the book

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula. For the parent who wants to ask better questions and stop spiraling on the answers from strangers.

Note on safety: this book names no specific medications and gives no medical advice. It exists to help you have a better conversation with the human who has access to your full chart.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography.
```
