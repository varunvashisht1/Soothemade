**Language:** English (United States)

**Book Title:**
> The Field Guide to Asking for Help

**Subtitle:**
> 30 Scripts for Asking Specific People for Specific Help. A Communication Companion for New Parents, Caregivers, and Anyone Carrying Too Much Alone.

**Series:** Soothemade · Book 2

**Author:** Soothemade

**Description:**

```
Most people will never ask for the help they need. They run themselves into the ground first, and then they say "I should have asked sooner," and then they do it again next time. The problem is rarely the ask. The problem is not knowing how.

This is the inverse of saying no. Thirty short scripts across eight categories of help — meals, childcare, eldercare, errands, emotional support, money, home help, visits — for the asks that most people never make.

Inside:

• The four-part structure of an ask that actually works: specific person, specific request, specific time, an easy exit for them
• 30 scripts you can copy into a text message right now
• What to say when they say no, without making it weird
• What to say when they say yes, without disappearing into gratitude
• A thank-you script library for after

Written by Maya, a postpartum doula. Plain language. No "you deserve this." No "ask the village" without telling you how. Just the words.

Part of the Soothemade bibliography. Slow, considered companions for the parts of life no one is photographing.
```
