# Brand Voice Guide

> Draft, written against **Option B (Maya, doula)** as the default. If Option A or C is picked, swap the "I" → "we" or "shop" and remove doula references. Tone rules apply across all three.

## The single sentence

**"A slightly-older friend who's been through it, telling you the truth no one else does — without preaching, without sanitizing, without selling you a fix."**

Every line we publish has to pass that bar.

## Tone calibration (the 5 dials)

| Dial | Setting | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | **Casual-warm, not slangy** | Contractions yes ("you're", "doesn't"). No "lol", no emojis except where structurally needed. |
| Authority | **Lived-in, not credentialed** | "Here's what worked for me" beats "Studies show". Cite expertise sparingly. |
| Emotional register | **Direct + tender** | Name the hard thing, then sit with it. Don't rush to a solution. |
| Optimism | **Honest, not toxic-positive** | Avoid "everything happens for a reason", "enjoy every moment", "this too shall pass." |
| Selling | **Quiet — let the work sell itself** | No urgency theater ("BUY NOW", "LAST CHANCE"). Soft CTAs. |

## Words to use

- *fourth trimester*, *postpartum*, *lochia*, *latch*, *let-down*, *engorgement* — clinical-precise where useful
- *gentle*, *slow*, *spacious*, *enough*, *ordinary*, *steady*, *small*, *quiet*
- *I made this when…*, *I wish someone had told me…*, *something that helped me…*
- *you*, *your* — talk *to* the reader, not about them

## Words to avoid

- *mama* (overused), *mommy* (infantilizing), *bump* (Instagram-coded), *little one* (overused), *kiddo*
- *journey* (used to death), *blessed*, *cherish*, *enjoy every moment*, *love language*, *living my best life*
- *crush it*, *#momlife*, *#blessed*
- *experts say*, *studies show*, *new research* — without citing
- *should*, *must*, *have to* — replace with *might want to*, *one option*, *if it helps*
- *boss mom*, *supermom*, *warrior mom*, *#girlmom*, *#boymom*

## Banned phrases (legal/safety lane)

- Anything that *prescribes* a sleep method, feeding schedule, or medical decision
- "Will help you lose weight" (postpartum body context)
- "Cures" / "treats" / "prevents" + any medical condition
- Any specific drug or supplement name as a recommendation
- "This is the only way" / "the right way" / "the wrong way"

## Sentence patterns

**Good:**
> The first time I cried into a microwave at 3am, I thought I was broken. Turns out, that's just Wednesday in the fourth trimester. This planner is what I made for the version of me who couldn't remember what day it was.

**Bad** (preachy):
> Postpartum recovery is challenging but rewarding. With this planner, you'll be empowered to take charge of your healing journey!

**Good** (soft sell):
> If a 5-week recovery planner — one that doesn't assume you bounced back by week 2 — sounds like a thing you'd use, here it is.

**Bad** (urgent sell):
> 🔥 BESTSELLER 🔥 Get your POSTPARTUM RECOVERY PLANNER NOW — limited time pricing!

## Structural rules

1. **First-line hook.** Every product description, blog post, email, and pin caption opens with a specific concrete observation, not a generality.
   - Yes: *"The visitor doorbell rang for the fourth time today and I started crying."*
   - No: *"Becoming a mom is challenging."*
2. **One idea per paragraph.** Postpartum brain doesn't track long paragraphs.
3. **Concrete > abstract.** Name the specific thing.
4. **Show your work in the product.** Reference real pages, real prompts — buyers want to know what they're getting.
5. **Disclaim once, clearly, in a footer.** Not in the body. *"This is a planning tool, not medical advice. If something feels wrong, call your provider."*

## Voice across surfaces

| Surface | Tone tweak |
|---|---|
| Etsy listing title | SEO-stuffed, dry, keyword-leading — voice doesn't apply here |
| Etsy listing description | First paragraph in voice; rest in scannable feature/benefit format |
| Gumroad sales page | Long-form voice; can be more vulnerable + storytelling |
| Blog post intro (first 100 words) | Most concentrated voice — the audition |
| Blog post body | Voice softens to informational |
| Pinterest pin overlay text | 5–9 words; pick the most-quotable line from the asset |
| Pinterest pin description | Voice-tight, 1–2 sentences, ends in soft hook |
| Email subject line | Casual specificity. *"the doorbell thing"* > *"How to handle visitors"* |
| Email body | Most voice-y surface; treat like a letter |

## Quality tests (apply before publish)

1. Would Maya/we/the brand actually say this out loud to a friend?
2. Did we name a specific thing, or only an abstract one?
3. Did we sell or did we show?
4. Did we presume the reader is competent, or did we condescend?
5. Is there any phrase here that could harm someone if they took it as medical advice?

If any answer is "wrong direction," rewrite.
