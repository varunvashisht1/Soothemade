# Brand Architecture — Soothemade

> The master brand + sub-brand model. Read this before naming any new product or line.

## The umbrella

**Soothemade** is the master brand. It owns the values, the voice, and the look. Everything we make falls under it.

The promise of Soothemade (across every line): *Slow, considered things to use through the unphotographed parts of life.*

The voice (across every line): *A slightly-older friend who's been through it. Warm-direct. Never preachy.* (Full guide in [`voice-guide.md`](./voice-guide.md).)

The visual identity (across every line): *Warm vintage. Ivory + terracotta + sage. Fraunces + Inter. Paper-textured, hand-drawn dividers, no drop shadows.* (Full guide in [`visual-identity.md`](./visual-identity.md).)

## Sub-brands (niche lines)

Each niche we expand into gets a sub-line under Soothemade. The pattern:

`Soothemade [Sub-line]` → `<sub-line>.soothemade.com`

### Active

| Sub-brand | Niche | Subdomain |
|---|---|---|
| **Soothemade Notes** | Printables + planners + cards for new parents | `notes.soothemade.com` |

### Reserved / future

| Sub-brand | Niche | Subdomain |
|---|---|---|
| **Soothemade Kitchen** | Recipe + meal-prep + freezer-meal printables | `kitchen.soothemade.com` |
| **Soothemade Studio** | Templates + workflows for solopreneurs / creators | `studio.soothemade.com` |
| **Soothemade Field** | Travel + outdoor + road-trip guides | `field.soothemade.com` |
| **Soothemade Press** | Long-form printed / digital books | `press.soothemade.com` |

These are reserved labels — don't launch any of them until research + signal warrants. The architecture exists so you don't have to rebrand later.

## What lives at `soothemade.com`

The root domain hosts an **umbrella site**: short, clean, mostly typographic. Explains who Soothemade is in 2–3 paragraphs, lists the active sub-lines with one-line descriptions, links out to each. No products live here directly.

Practical effect: a buyer who lands on `notes.soothemade.com` for a postpartum planner can see the brand also offers a Kitchen line later, and the cross-pollination is automatic.

## Etsy + Gumroad architecture

**One Etsy shop**: `Soothemade`. Internal Etsy sections become sub-lines:

| Etsy section | Maps to |
|---|---|
| Notes | Soothemade Notes (current) |
| Kitchen | Soothemade Kitchen (future) |
| Studio | Soothemade Studio (future) |

**Gumroad**: same — single creator account `gumroad.com/soothemade`, products tagged by sub-line.

Don't open separate Etsy shops per line until any single line clears ~$2k/month — then it's worth the operational overhead.

## Voice differentiation between sub-lines

Voice stays the same brand voice across all lines. What changes is:

- **Audience-specific vocabulary** — Notes uses "fourth trimester," "lochia." Kitchen would use "freezer meal," "make-ahead." Studio would use "launch week," "subscribers."
- **Example scenarios** — Notes opens with the 3am scenario. Kitchen would open with "the Tuesday night when there's nothing in the fridge."
- **Specific products referenced** — each line cross-links primarily within itself

What stays identical: tone calibration, banned phrases, structural rules.

## Visual differentiation between sub-lines

Same palette, same typography, same texture rules. What can vary:

- **Accent emphasis** — Notes leans terracotta + sage. Kitchen might lean wheat + sage. Studio might lean stone + terracotta.
- **Iconography motifs** — Notes might use hand-drawn leaves. Kitchen might use hand-drawn kitchen items. The drawing *style* stays the same.

Don't add new fonts. Don't change the brand voice. Don't reach for new color families.

## When to create a new sub-brand

A new sub-brand makes sense when:

1. You have **clear research signal** that the niche has demand (Pinterest trend, Etsy gap, customer requests).
2. The audience is **clearly distinct** from existing lines (a Kitchen buyer might or might not also be a Notes buyer — that's fine, but they should feel they're in a related-but-separate room).
3. You have **at least 5 product ideas** that would fit the sub-brand. (Don't launch a line for one product.)
4. The launch won't dilute current line attention for at least 2 quarters.

If any of these is "no," wait.

## What this prevents

The whole point of sub-brand architecture is:

- **You can pivot the current niche without rebranding.** If "Notes" stalls, you can launch "Kitchen" next year without scrapping the whole shop.
- **Cross-customer flow.** A buyer who loves a Notes product can later buy a Kitchen product from a brand they already trust.
- **Asset reuse.** Voice, visual identity, persona, publishing system, toolkit — all carry over to every new line.
- **Better sale potential.** A multi-line group brand sells for higher multiples than a single-niche shop on Flippa / Empire Flippers (if you ever sell).

## Don't do

- **Don't launch a niche with a totally different aesthetic.** That's a new brand, not a sub-line. Spin it as a separate entity if so.
- **Don't dilute the master brand with too many lines.** 2–3 active lines is sustainable. 5+ becomes unfocused.
- **Don't use sub-brand names that don't follow the pattern.** Keep it `Soothemade <Single Noun>`.
- **Don't redirect `soothemade.com` to a single niche.** It stays an umbrella forever.
