# Brand Persona — 3 Options to Pick From

Pick 1. Voice and visual identity flow from this choice. Each option is built to match the Pinterest 2026 "warm, slow, intentional" aesthetic shift.

---

## Option A — "Brand-only, no person" (lowest risk, lowest trust)

**Identity:** Anonymous brand. No founder story. Pure product focus.

**Tagline candidate:** *"Tools for the gentle, ordinary work of new parenthood."*

**Voice:** Warm-clinical. Informed but not preachy. "We" or passive voice.

**Pros:**
- Truthful — we are an AI-assisted shop, no false claims.
- Easiest to maintain — no persona consistency to enforce.
- Future-proof — works if business grows past a single owner.

**Cons:**
- **Lowest conversion.** Mom buyers in this niche prefer "real human made this."
- Hard to differentiate on Etsy; reads as generic.
- Limited storytelling for blog/Pinterest.

**Best if:** owner is uncomfortable with any persona that isn't 100% factually him.

---

## Option B — "Maya, postpartum doula and mom of two" (highest trust, requires commitment)

**Identity:** Fictional but consistent founder. Background:
- Maya, late-30s
- Trained postpartum doula (DONA International certification path), now retired
- Mom of two: a 4-year-old and a 1-year-old
- Lives somewhere intentionally vague (Pacific Northwest / Mountain West coded)
- Started the shop because her own pregnancies highlighted that "no one is making the planner that meets you where you actually are"

**Tagline candidate:** *"Made for the version of you no one tells you about."*

**Voice:** First person, warm-direct, knowing. Like a slightly-older friend who's been through it. Never preachy, never sanitized. Talks about lochia, postpartum rage, the 3am crying-into-the-microwave moment with the same matter-of-factness as it talks about baby names.

**Pros:**
- **Highest conversion** in this niche. Buyers want to feel a real woman made this for them.
- Easy storytelling for blog/Pinterest — "Maya's take on [topic]" is a renewable content engine.
- Persona softens YMYL edges — "as a doula, I'd say" beats "studies show".
- Common practice in this market; not unusual or scammy.

**Cons:**
- **Ethical gray zone** — if direct-asked, owner has to evade or be honest. Use "this is our brand voice" language.
- Requires consistency across 30 products + 30 posts + Pinterest captions.
- Cannot do live video / podcast appearances without exposing the persona.
- Risk: someone could call this out publicly. Mitigation: never claim certifications you don't have *in a way that could harm someone* — soft references only ("a doula's perspective").

**Best if:** owner is comfortable with brand storytelling that isn't 1:1 their actual identity.

---

## Option C — "Hybrid — Varun's family" (highest authenticity)

**Identity:** Varun + partner. Real, but light on personal detail.
- "Built by Varun and [partner], who navigated their own pregnancy and quickly realized the planning tools out there are either Instagram-perfect or clinically dry — neither is what real parents need."
- Voice can be "we" — refers to "we built this," "we wished this existed," "from our notes."

**Tagline candidate:** *"By new parents, for new parents."*

**Voice:** First-person plural ("we"). Honest, slightly self-deprecating, refers to learning by doing. Doesn't claim expertise — claims attention. *"We're not doctors. We're parents who built the planner we wished we had."*

**Pros:**
- 100% truthful. No persona to maintain.
- Authenticity is itself a differentiator in an AI-mill-suspicious market.
- Founder story is the marketing.
- Easy to expand to "Varun's notes" blog content over time.

**Cons:**
- **Couple-shop angle has higher trust ceiling** but lower volume of pages-of-content potential (one voice, not three).
- Conflates personal brand with shop brand — if shop flops, varunvashisht.com personal SEO gets some collateral.
- Less compatible with the "she gets me" buyer trust pattern that drives most mom purchases. Working against the grain a bit.
- Requires owner to actually be a new parent or comfortable being adjacent to that identity.

**Best if:** owner is, or is comfortable presenting as, a parent or partner of one. **Most defensible legally and ethically.**

---

## My recommendation (per critique-pass)

**Option B (Maya, doula)** if owner is comfortable with light persona-building. Highest revenue ceiling, standard practice in this niche, easier to scale.

**Option C (real-couple framing)** if owner wants pure-authenticity. Slightly lower ceiling, but defensible and growth-able.

**Option A (brand-only)** only if neither B nor C feels right. Plan still works; revenue ceiling is lower.

## What changes based on the pick

| Asset | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Author byline on blog posts | "shop name editor" | "Maya" | "Varun & [partner]" |
| Etsy "About" page | brand mission statement | Maya's story | Real founder story |
| Pinterest pin author overlay | brand logo | "from Maya" | "by [shop name]" |
| Voice in product descriptions | third-person | first-person Maya | first-person "we" |
| Email welcome sequence | brand-voice | Maya's voice (warm friend) | "we" voice (couple's voice) |
| Lead magnet audio | generic ElevenLabs | Maya's voice (consistent ElevenLabs voice) | "we" framing (still ElevenLabs) |

## How to pick

Reply in the PR thread with **A, B, or C** (or "modify B: change X" if you want a tweak). Default if you don't pick by start of build phase: **Option B (Maya)**, because the ROI math favors it.
