# The Soothemade Story — umbrella

> The master narrative for `soothemade.com`. All umbrella copy should ladder back to this. The Notes voice guide still applies; this widens it from parenthood-specific to the master-brand level.

## The single sentence

**Soothemade is a small studio making slow, considered things for the quiet parts of life no one is photographing.**

Every line on the umbrella site has to pass that bar.

## What we mean by "the quiet parts"

The hour before anyone else is up. The walk you took because you couldn't sit still. The recipe you cooked on a Tuesday when no one was coming over. The page you wrote and didn't show anybody. The pregnancy week between appointments. The grief you didn't post about. The Saturday you spent doing exactly nothing on purpose.

Most of a life is made of these hours. The ones with the photographs in them are the foam on top.

We make things — mostly paper things — for the hours under the foam.

## What we mean by "made slowly"

The kind of slow that a tomato is. The kind of slow that a recovery is. The kind of slow that learning anything actually is, before someone sold you a course that promised otherwise.

We make planners, journals, printables, and card decks. They are researched by a human, voiced by a human, designed by a human, and read end-to-end by a human before they ship. The tools we use to make them faster are tools, the way a sewing machine is a tool. They do not write your letter and they do not decide what you need.

We make one thing at a time. We finish it. We make the next one.

## What we mean by "loving yourself"

We don't, actually. We never use the phrase. The wellness industry used it up.

What we mean is: giving the quiet version of yourself good tools. A page to write on that doesn't yell at you. A planner that doesn't pretend you bounce back. A card deck that asks one question instead of selling you twelve. The opposite of bath bombs and affirmations. The same thing, said in less light.

If "loving yourself" means anything that hasn't been ruined, it probably means this: noticing the unphotographed parts of yourself, and treating them as if they were the real ones. Because they are.

## What we mean by "nature"

Pace.

Things in nature don't have launch dates. A tomato doesn't crush its Q3. A pregnancy doesn't optimize. The light at six in the morning doesn't get any faster if you yell at it. Nature is just a long-running argument that some things take exactly as long as they take, and most of human suffering comes from refusing to believe that.

We try to make things at the pace of weather. Some weeks we ship something. Some weeks we don't. It evens out the way a season does.

## What we mean by "peaceful"

A posture, not an aesthetic. The peace of having one good thing to do instead of seventeen okay things. Of one page open in front of you instead of a dashboard. Of a paper card you hold for a minute, instead of a notification you swipe in two seconds.

Our peace looks like restraint. White space, one accent color per section, no exclamation points, no urgency theater. The peace is in how little is on the page.

## The small house, with rooms

Soothemade is a small house with one room finished and a few more being built.

The first room is **Soothemade Notes** — printables, planners, journals, and cards for new parents. The 3 a.m. parts, the postpartum body, the visitor doorbell, the recovery month nobody warned you about.

The other rooms — for the home cook, for the solo worker, for the long traveler — are still being made. Slowly. By the same hands. When they open, you'll be the first to know if you've left an address.

## What we are not

- A wellness brand. We don't think you have a self to optimize. We think you have a life to live, and some of it is plain, and we make things for the plain parts.
- A subscription. You buy a printable, you keep it forever, you owe us nothing else.
- A megaphone. Soothemade is small on purpose. If it gets larger, the voice you read here is what we are accountable to.

## Where the name comes from

It's "soothe" plus "-made." It rhymes with "homemade" because that's the register. The things in this shop are made — slowly, by hand, with the unphotographed days in mind.

## Anchor lines

The lines that should appear, in some form, across the site:

- "Slow, considered things for the seasons of a life."
- "Made for the parts of life no one is photographing."
- "We make things at the pace of weather."
- "The hours under the foam are the real ones."
- "A small house. One room open. More being built."

## Words to use

Same as Notes voice guide, plus umbrella-level:

- *slow*, *quiet*, *unphotographed*, *spacious*, *enough*, *ordinary*, *steady*, *small*, *plain*
- *weather*, *season*, *pace*, *light*, *room*, *house*, *hour*, *Tuesday*
- *made*, *finished*, *put down*

## Words to avoid

Same as Notes voice guide, plus:

- *wellness*, *self-care* (the phrase, not the concept), *journey*, *intentional living*, *mindful*, *holistic*, *manifest*, *abundance*
- *empower*, *unlock*, *transform*, *level up*, *thrive*
- *touch grass*, *grounded*, *aligned*, *the wisdom of [nature thing]*
- *crush*, *optimize*, *hack*, *system*, *workflow* (for self-directed contexts)
- "✨", any emoji as ornament

## Tagline (locked)

**Made for the parts of life no one is photographing.**

Works as H1, footer line, social bio, email signature. Don't reword.
